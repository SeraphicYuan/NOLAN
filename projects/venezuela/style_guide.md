# Style Guide: Venezuela Documentary

A polished, accessible-but-rigorous video essay on the roots of Venezuela's modern divisions. Aimed at a general audience curious about how an oil-rich nation became fractured — depth without academic distance, analysis without partisan posture. Think explainer-documentary hybrid: cinematic look, structured argument, plain language.

## Voice

Conversational essayist. Addresses the viewer directly ("we're going to explore today," "you might think," "we've taken a look"). Walks the line between accessible and rigorous: complex history (caudillos, the 1976 nationalization, the Caracazo, the Bolivarian Revolution) made approachable through plain language and recurring structural cues. Educational posture, not advocacy — the script is explicit about this ("this isn't a political statement — it is an analysis of the causes behind the crisis").

Recurring rhetorical moves used in this script:
- Open on a sharp paradox: the country's natural beauty and oil wealth set against poverty, shortages, and unrest
- Quote a named real person early for human texture — Maria Rodriguez, a Caracas resident, in the hook. Use her quote once; do not invent additional speakers
- Number the thesis explicitly: "three lenses — first... second... finally" (colonial legacy / oil dependence / political fragmentation). Mirror this numbering visually at each Evidence section opening
- Cadence phrase: **"here's the key insight"** (also rendered as "here is the key insight"). The script uses it five times — at the thesis, in Evidence 1, Evidence 2, Evidence 3, and the conclusion. Treat each instance as a flagged beat that earns a dedicated visual marker
- Close on reflection and an open-ended call to action — dialogue, reconciliation, sustainable growth — not a partisan verdict

## Look

Cinematic, color-graded, premium-feel documentary. Combine four modes, weighted toward the indexed library since this project has substantial real footage to work with:

- **Real footage from the indexed library** — the three indexed source videos ("Hugo Chavez — Venezuela's Savior or Destroyer," "Venezuela's Dark Secret — The History They Don't Teach," "Why The US & Venezuela Are On The Brink of War") provide archival and contemporary documentary footage covering Chávez-era rallies, Caracas streetscapes, oil infrastructure, protest scenes, Maduro-era news footage, and Venezuelan landscapes. Lean on this material for the Hook, Evidence 2, and Evidence 3 sections especially
- **AI-generated conceptual imagery** for moments without library coverage: pre-Columbian Arawak/Carib settlements, Bolívar-era independence battles, the early 20th-century discovery of oil, Juan Vicente Gómez and José Antonio Páez portraiture, symbolic compositions of "society split down the middle" or "a house of cards economy." Prompts should lean cinematic and photorealistic — period-accurate dress, era-correct architecture
- **Text overlays** — Maria Rodriguez's quote, each "here's the key insight" beat, section markers ("First / Second / Finally"), and the closing call-to-action. Typography-driven, clean, restrained color palette
- **Information graphics** — a Venezuela map showing Arawak (west) / Carib (east) territories; an oil-price-vs-GDP timeline spanning the 1970s boom, 1980s collapse, 1989 Caracazo, 1998 Chávez election, 2014 oil crash; a hyperinflation chart for the Maduro era; a simple three-card "lenses" graphic introduced at the thesis and reused as section openers

Avoid stock-footage-look b-roll where indexed archival material is available. Symbolic compositions for political moments are encouraged; political caricature of named figures (Chávez, Maduro, Guaidó, Bolívar) is not — keep them dignified, period-appropriate, neutral.

## Pacing

Total runtime 9:50 (590s) — squarely in the template's tuned ~10-minute zone. Average scene length 8–12s, varied by section:

- **Hook (0:00–0:52, 52s)** — fast, ~6–7s avg, ~8 scenes. Open on landscape beauty, hard cut to crisis imagery, then the Maria Rodriguez quote, then the oil-reserves paradox
- **Context (0:52–2:18, 86s)** — standard ~10s avg. Indigenous-era and colonial-era beats need generated imagery; the oil-discovery turn benefits from a graphic transition
- **Thesis (2:18–3:10, 52s)** — ~10s avg, but the "three lenses" enumeration earns a dedicated graphic card (~6–8s) plus a held text-overlay on "here is the key insight"
- **Evidence 1 (3:10–4:58, 108s)** — ~10s avg. Caudillos era leans generated-image + map graphics; Páez and Gómez get portrait-style generated stills; Betancourt and the 1976 nationalization can use library footage where available
- **Evidence 2 (4:58–6:48, 110s)** — ~10s avg. The 1970s boom, 1980s austerity, 1989 Caracazo, and Chávez's 1998 rise are well-covered by the indexed library — lean heavily on b-roll here
- **Evidence 3 (6:48–8:19, 91s)** — ~10s avg. 2014 oil crash, hyperinflation, migration, Maduro, Guaidó — all in the indexed library
- **Conclusion (8:19–9:50, 91s)** — slower, 12–15s avg. Synthesis needs to land; final "here's the key insight" gets a held text-overlay; close on a reflective generated-image or landscape b-roll

Visual-type variety is enforced: no more than 2 consecutive scenes of the same `visual_type`. Each section transition gets a distinct visual marker — for Evidence 1/2/3, reuse the "lenses" card with the active lens highlighted.

## Editorial

- Use the Maria Rodriguez quote once, in the Hook, as a dedicated text-overlay scene. Do not add quotes from other named individuals — the script doesn't include them, and inventing them would breach the project's analytic posture
- The Context and Evidence 1 sections lean heavily on maps, timelines, and generated period imagery — pre-1900 Venezuela has no library footage, so build it visually through graphics and AI-generated stills
- Each "here's the key insight" beat (5 occurrences) gets a dedicated text-overlay scene. The phrase is the script's spine; treat it as a structural device, not narration to gloss over
- The "three lenses" thesis is multi-part — open Evidence 1, Evidence 2, and Evidence 3 with a small graphic card naming the active lens (1. Colonial Legacy & Caudillos / 2. Oil Dependence & Inequality / 3. Political Fragmentation)
- Avoid sustained talking-heads from the indexed library — when a clip from a source documentary shows a presenter speaking, intercut quickly with relevant imagery
- Match visual register to the script's non-partisan posture. Chávez and Maduro footage should be sourced from neutral-context moments (rallies, official addresses) rather than caricatured or hostile framings. The script analyzes; the visuals analyze
- The Hook's "land of incredible beauty" beat benefits from real Venezuelan landscape footage — Angel Falls, the Gran Sabana, Caribbean coastline — drawn from the indexed library where available, generated otherwise

## Visual Type Vocabulary

The four modes used by this project. No extensions needed — the source material is conventional documentary subject matter.

- `b-roll` — real footage from the three indexed source videos (Chávez-era rallies, Caracas streets, oil infrastructure, protests, Maduro-era news, Venezuelan landscapes)
- `generated-image` — AI-generated stills for pre-photographic eras (Arawak/Carib settlements, Bolívar's independence campaigns, Páez and Gómez portraiture) and symbolic compositions (a divided society, a house-of-cards economy)
- `text-overlay` — Maria Rodriguez's quote; each "here's the key insight" beat; the "First / Second / Finally" section markers; the closing call-to-action
- `graphic` — Venezuela indigenous-territories map; oil-price/GDP timeline; hyperinflation chart; the three-lenses card reused as section openers

Target distribution for this project, tuned to source-library availability: **45% b-roll, 25% generated-image, 18% text-overlay, 12% graphic**. The generated-image share is slightly elevated versus the template default because pre-1900 Venezuela has no archival coverage and demands fabricated period imagery; the text-overlay share is elevated to support the five "key insight" beats and the explicit thesis enumeration.
