## CONTEXT beat — the boom is real and probably unstoppable (steel-man the pro side)

- The buildout is "the next industrial revolution": OpenAI/Oracle/SoftBank's "Stargate" aims to invest "trillions with a T"; Nvidia pledged **$100 billion** toward OpenAI. — src:[S5] · purpose:authority · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- Big tech is projected to spend **more than $800 billion** on AI infrastructure in **2026**, exceeding **$1 trillion in 2027** — "on par with the entire US defense budget." — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- By **2030**, companies are estimated to have invested **nearly $7 trillion** in data-center infrastructure. — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports

## EVIDENCE beat — where the bill lands (the concentrated local costs)

- **Your power bill:** Virginia's Dominion Energy disclosed it must roughly **double** its electricity generation by **2039**, mainly for data centers + EVs; the expansion could cost up to **$103 billion**, raising residential bills **by as much as 50%**. — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Priced out entirely:** Lake Tahoe residents' only fallbacks are buying wholesale power at "two, three times as much at a minimum," or waiting years for a new transmission line — and California regulators can't order a Nevada utility to serve them. — src:[S2] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Water in the desert:** up to **43% of the largest data centers** sit in areas of high or extremely high water stress. — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- A planned Microsoft cluster in Arizona (280 generators): each building planned for **1 million gallons of water a day** — **1.83 billion gallons a year** — "enough water for roughly **61,000 Americans** or a city the size of **Santa Cruz, California**" — drawing from the Colorado River, whose flow is down 20% since 2000. — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **The absurd detail:** a QTS site in Aurora, CO uses **more water to irrigate its lawn** (1.1M gal/yr) than to cool its servers — "double the amount used by the building itself." — src:[S1] · purpose:contrast · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **The noise you can feel:** Carlos Janis spent **$20,000** on insulation and new windows and still can't sleep; "you can even hear the windows vibrate"; he moved his whole family to the basement. REBUTTAL same line: "An Amazon spokesperson told us the centers operate well below required sound levels." — src:[S1] · purpose:humanize · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- The American Public Health Association says chronic noise exposure can lead to serious health problems like **cardiovascular disease** or increased stress. — src:[S1] · purpose:authority · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Dirty power under the "green" pledge:** OpenAI's Stargate (Texas) already has **62 diesel generators and 10 gas turbines**, plans for 41 more — becoming "one of the largest fossil-fuel power plants of any kind in Texas," emitting **>7.8 million tons** of greenhouse gases/year (≈2 million cars). — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Texas has ~**80 GW** of gas power under construction; roughly **half** will serve data centers exclusively, "behind the meter," **off the public grid**. Renewables won't power AI "at any real scale" before **2028**; new nuclear is "a decade or more away." — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Seized land:** eminent domain is being used for data-center transmission lines. Ansley (Coweta County, GA): "This is my childhood home behind me. It is being taken by force by Georgia Power." More than a dozen Kentucky farmers were threatened with eminent domain (the Guardian); a Virginia farmer, Todd Lax, says Dominion offered ~**$1,500/acre** for land he values far higher. — src:[S2] · purpose:humanize · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **The kicker — almost no jobs:** even the largest data centers employ **fewer than 150** permanent workers, "some as few as 25." Abilene's Stargate: ~**1,500** building it, but only ~**100** full-time once open — vs a same-footprint cheese-packaging plant projected to employ **500**. — src:[S1,S2] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports

## CLOSE / CALIBRATION beat — steel-man both, land a fair verdict, human stakes

- **The panic is overblown (scale check):** nationally, all data-center water withdrawals "account for about **5% of the water usage of golf courses**" — the water problem is *local*, not national. — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges
- Data centers **don't** cause blackouts ("grids plan years ahead"); diesel stacks are backup-only; not all are "water guzzlers" — designs range from air-cooling to closed-loop. — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges
- Meta runs a Denmark site that **exports 100–165 GWh of waste heat a year** into a district-heating system — proof the downsides aren't inevitable. — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges
- **But the boosters dodge too:** "water positive by 2030" pledges rely on "an elaborate system of water credits or offsetting" — paying others to save water, not using less on site. Same for carbon. — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Jevons paradox** (William Stanley Jevons, 1860s coal): efficiency gains don't cut total use — cheaper, more efficient AI just drives more demand, erasing the savings. — src:[S4] · purpose:authority · beat:close · conf:verified · role:context
- The skeptic's own bottom line: "Does that mean we should never build any data centers… abandon AI entirely? Of course not" — but don't race "with no guardrails… based on some vague notion that we need to beat China." — src:[S2] · purpose:authority · beat:close · conf:verified · role:context
- **The human close:** OG (Omaira Garcia), Abilene veteran next to Stargate: "We invest in our home, we serve our country, and we weren't even included in this process… I feel trapped." — src:[S4] · purpose:humanize · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- Donna Gallant (Prince William County, 30 years on her street): "I can only fight for so long. And then I'm going to wave the white flag and I'm going to pack up and I'm going to leave… It breaks my heart." — src:[S1] · purpose:humanize · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
