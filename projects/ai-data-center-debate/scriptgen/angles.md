# Candidate angles — AI Data Center Debate

**Inferred spine type (from the style guide):** a **data-grounded, both-sides argument**. Every exemplar takes one sharp question — a candidate, a race, a threat, a counterfactual — and walks it with the manner of "a smart friend who did the reading": pin every claim to a number/date/dollar figure, steel-man the opposing case fairly, explicitly separate "what I'd personally prefer" from "what's actually likely," hedge honestly, and land a *calibrated* verdict (often "they're probably both right to some degree"). This is NOT a human-thematic braid and NOT a dry how-it-works explainer. So the mechanism material (S3/S5) is *grounding*, and the loudest activism (S2's council clip) is a *hook*, not the thesis.

**The trap to avoid:** the guide's #1 DON'T is "don't preach a single viewpoint or perform outrage." A one-sided "data centers are evil" screed is off-type even though the harm footage (S1/S4) is vivid. The right angle holds both sides on real numbers and reaches a fair verdict.

---

### Angle 1 — It's not AI vs. no-AI. It's *who pays* — and the bill is landing on the people with the least say.  **[CHOSEN]**
- *thesis:* The AI buildout is real and basically unstoppable. The actual, winnable argument is about **cost allocation**: the benefits are diffuse (all of us use AI, Big Tech profits, a few counties bank tax revenue) while the costs are hyper-concentrated (higher power bills, drained desert water, seized land, a hum you feel in the walls, and almost no permanent jobs) on specific host communities that mostly never got a vote.
- *core (the contrast):* diffuse benefit vs. concentrated cost — plus the transparency gap (NDAs, shell LLCs, a "permit by rule" you'd hand a dry cleaner) that lets it happen quietly.
- *supported by:* [S1] map, bills, jobs, water · [S2] Lake Tahoe, eminent domain, shell companies · [S4] Stargate, permit-by-rule, regulator quote · [S5] Loudoun's 38% (the pro side), myth-busting · [S3] scale.
- *why a viewer cares:* it converts a trillion-dollar abstraction into their electric bill, their home value, and whether anyone bothered to ask them.
- **Resonance 5/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 5/5**
- *Why it wins:* it IS the channel's signature machine — separate preference from what's-actually-likely, steel-man both sides, follow the money, land a calibrated verdict. It turns the loudest hysteria (Angle 3) into the cold-open hook and absorbs Angles 2, 4 and 5 as body segments. Deeply resonant without preaching.

### Angle 2 — Nobody voted on this: the biggest buildout of our lifetime is happening with less public input than a new dry cleaner.
- *thesis:* The core scandal is procedural — secrecy, shell companies, fast-track permits — not the technology.
- *core:* consent/transparency as the fault line (no public directory, redacted trade-secret records, permit-by-rule).
- *supported by:* [S1] no public directory · [S2] shell LLCs · [S4] permit-by-rule + overwhelmed regulator.
- *why a viewer cares:* consent-of-the-governed is a genuinely bipartisan principle.
- **Resonance 4/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 5/5**
- *Note:* excellent and perfectly on-type, but narrower — it's really the *turn* inside Angle 1. Folded in as the sharpest body segment, not the whole spine.

### Angle 3 — The hysteria is wrong — and that's exactly what Big Tech is counting on.
- *thesis:* Apocalyptic fear-mongering (poisoned air, whole counties wiped out) discredits the real, narrower problems and hands Big Tech an easy villain.
- *core:* separate legitimate concern from panic (national water ≈ 5% of golf courses; no blackouts; myths debunked).
- *supported by:* [S2] anti-hysteria argument · [S5] myth-busting.
- *why a viewer cares:* it flatters the viewer's wish to be the reasonable one in the room.
- **Resonance 4/5** · **Evidence 4/5** · **Style-fit 5/5** (literally the channel's steel-man posture)
- *Penalized as a spine:* as the thesis it becomes a media-criticism/meta beat and risks reading as dismissive of genuinely harmed residents. Best used as the HOOK's second move + the calibration beat — "counter-source as hook, not thesis."

### Angle 4 — Green promises, gas reality: the AI sold as a climate fix runs on some of the dirtiest power we've got.
- *thesis:* Renewable/nuclear pledges vs. what's actually being built — natural gas, coal plants kept open, diesel and turbines, Jevons paradox.
- *core:* promise vs. reality (a classic channel micro-arc).
- *supported by:* [S4] gas buildout + Jevons · [S1] utilities reversing net-zero · [S3] grid still fossil-heavy.
- **Resonance 3/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 4/5**
- *Note:* strong data but single-issue (environment) and closest to the "preach one viewpoint" trap. One body segment inside Angle 1, not the spine.

### Angle 5 — Can the grid take it — and who picks up a $100 billion tab?
- *thesis:* Grid strain + cost pass-through to ordinary ratepayers is the sleeper story.
- *core:* who pays for the upgrades (Dominion's $103B / +50% bills; Lake Tahoe priced out entirely).
- *supported by:* [S1] Dominion · [S2] Lake Tahoe · [S5] PJM/DOE demand growth.
- **Resonance 4/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 5/5**
- *Note:* the "your bill" half of Angle 1. Folded in as a body segment.

---

**Winner: Angle 1.** It wins on **resonance** (it reframes a trillion-dollar abstraction as your bill, your home, and whether anyone asked you) and on **style-fit** (it is the channel's exact both-sides / follow-the-money / preference-vs-likelihood machine). It uses the loudest activism as the hook, steel-mans the genuine pro-build case (Loudoun's revenue and tax cuts, the myth-busting, Denmark's waste-heat reuse, the golf-course water stat), and lands a calibrated verdict instead of a sermon.
