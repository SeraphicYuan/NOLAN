# Beat-map — AI Data Center Debate  (constitution = channel-stickman-talks/style_guide.md)

**Spine + arc:** The fight isn't AI vs. no-AI — it's *who pays*. Benefits are diffuse (we all use AI; Big Tech profits; a few counties bank tax revenue); costs are concentrated on host communities that mostly never got a vote (higher bills, drained water, seized land, a hum you feel, almost no jobs) — and a transparency gap lets it happen quietly. Contrast = **diffuse benefit vs. concentrated cost.** It rides the guide's beat sequence: **Hook → Intro/ground-rules + soft CTA → [sponsor: SKIPPED] → segmented body (setup→data→verdict→transition each) → both-sides weighing → story/emotion close → sign-off (adapted).**

## Craft-vs-clothing decisions (handled once, here)
- **SKELETON** (Hook types, Narrative Structure, Pacing arc, DON'Ts): honored fully.
- **MUSCLE** (Rhetorical Devices + Sentence-level Style + Transitions): honored fully — each beat below commits named devices.
- **SKIN** (diction/register: plain English, contractions, fillers, measured heat): honored by ear.
- **CLOTHING:**
  - **Sponsor read + coded URL (`ground.news/stickman`) → SKIPPED entirely.** Not written. (Task hard rule; and we don't want the ad.)
  - **Persona label ("I'm literally a stickman") → ADAPTED, not copied.** Take its *function* — self-deprecation that disarms before a polarizing take — and render it neutrally ("I'm just some guy who read the permit filings so you don't have to"). Never claim the channel's literal mascot identity.
  - **Catchphrase sign-off ("That's all I've got… take care") → ADAPTED to a neutral functional equivalent** (a warm wrap + genuine like/subscribe + "sources are in the description" + invite-disagreement), without transplanting the verbatim catchphrase or the "channel members / Substack" furniture.
  - **Exemplar Lines → cadence tuning-fork only.** Match rhythm ("They're probably both right to some degree."; "Picture this."), never transplant a political line.

**Retention rules imported from THIS guide (HARD):** hook = why-this-matters-now + a hard fact/quote in the first breath; intro restates the topic, sets the trust rule (separate preference from likelihood), soft like/subscribe; segmented body, each a micro-arc (setup → data → verdict → spoken transition); **signpost every seam with a spoken cue** ("Now," "Here's the thing," "Let's start with," "Now let's zoom out," "Moving on though") and recap-then-tease; DATA-GROUND everything; STEEL-MAN before judging; HEDGE ("on paper," "practically speaking," "to some degree"); LABEL speculation as a thought experiment; **describe outrage, don't perform it**; no isolated analysis-only beats; **no numbered "First/Second" signposting** (spoken cues are fine — that's this channel's device). Back third stays story/emotion-forward. Target ~1200 words.

## Hook · pace:d · covers:[S2,S1] · serves-spine: open on the single most concrete, dated shock, then reframe within two sentences into the *who-pays* question. Lead with Lake Tahoe (documented, dated, quotable) BEFORE the council-meeting rhetoric.
· **devices:** Data-grounding (dated hard fact first) · second-person / stakes-setting punch · rhetorical question as pivot · quote-marking ("quote… unquote").
· **anchors:** "NV Energy is cutting power to the Lake Tahoe region after **May 2027** — it needs the capacity for data centers — leaving ~**49,000** people to find electricity somewhere else." Danielle Hughes VERBATIM: "It's like we don't exist." Then the scale beat: "more than **two** new data centers a week"; "a single one can pull more power than **half a million homes** (by some estimates)." Pivot line: "The real question isn't whether these things are scary. It's who's actually paying for them."

## Intro / ground-rules + soft CTA · pace:d · covers:[S2] · serves-spine: restate the question, set the channel's trust rule out loud, disarm both sides, soft like/subscribe.
· **devices:** trust posture ("I'll be straight with you") · preference-vs-likelihood rule stated aloud · self-deprecation (ADAPTED, neutral) · bipartisan address · spoken fillers ("Look," "Now").
· **anchors:** ground rule VERBATIM-in-function: "I'm going to separate what I'd personally prefer from what's actually likely, and steel-man both sides before I land anywhere." Adapted self-deprecation: "I'm just some guy who read the permit filings so you don't have to." Bipartisan: "Booster or abolitionist, stick around — the honest version is more interesting than either team wants." Soft CTA: "hit like and subscribe, it genuinely helps."

## Segment A — "The boom is real" (steel-man the pro side) · pace:a · covers:[S5,S4,S1,S3] · serves-spine: establish scale/legitimacy so the piece isn't anti-tech, then tease the catch.
· **devices:** Data-grounding (rapid number cluster — accelerate) · contrast/juxtaposition · colloquial hyperbole for color ("industrial-revolution money") · rhetorical question · short-declarative verdict.
· **anchors:** "**$800 billion** in 2026, north of **$1 trillion** in 2027 — basically the entire US defense budget, on server farms." Demand: "chatbot users doubled in a year — **400 to 900 million**." Power: "**4.4%** of US electricity in 2023 → **7–12% by 2028**"; "one AI prompt ≈ **10×** a Google search." The pro-side comparator: "In **Loudoun County, Virginia**, data centers throw off about **38%** of the county budget — enough they actually *cut* property taxes." Verdict (short): "We are not un-building this thing."

## Segment B — "So where does the bill land?" (the concentrated costs) · pace:a→d · covers:[S1,S2,S4] · serves-spine: the heart — rapid data roll, each cost cashed against "who pays," decelerate for the human noise beat.
· **devices:** Data-grounding · contrast (diffuse benefit ↔ concentrated cost) · second-person scene-setting ("Picture this") · in-beat rebuttal (Amazon's on-the-record denial kept on the line) · vivid concrete comparator · short-declarative kicker.
· **anchors:** Your bill: "Dominion must roughly **double** generation by **2039**… up to **$103 billion**… residential bills up **as much as 50%**." Water: "up to **43%** of the biggest sit in high water stress; one Arizona Microsoft campus planned **1.83 billion gallons a year** — enough for **~61,000 people**, a city the size of **Santa Cruz**." Noise (decelerate, "Picture this"): "Carlos spent **$20,000** on windows and still couldn't sleep — his **seven-year-old** had nightmares about a **spaceship** outside" + REBUTTAL on the line: "Amazon says the centers run well below required sound levels." Land: "in Georgia and Kentucky some folks are losing their land to **eminent domain**." Kicker (short): "And these places don't even get the jobs — the biggest sites run on **fewer than 150** people, some as few as **25**. Stargate in Texas: about **100** full-time."

## Segment C — "And nobody really voted on it" (the turn) · pace:a · covers:[S1,S2,S4] · serves-spine: the sharpest, most resonant beat — consent/transparency. Deploy the controversy as an accelerated fact-cluster, never a lecture.
· **devices:** Data-grounding · historical analogy/precedent (Disney shells) · the killer-detail contrast (dry cleaner ↔ city-sized plant) · authority quote · rhetorical question · anticipatory rebuttal ("this isn't Luddites panicking").
· **anchors:** "No public list of where these are or who owns them — reporters reverse-engineered a **map out of backup-generator permits** because everything's redacted as a trade secret." Shells: "Google bought land through a Delaware shell called **Jetstream**; Meta locked a **$60M**, 15-year tax deal in Ohio as **'Sicat'** before anyone knew it was Facebook." Precedent: "**Disney** pulled the same trick in the '60s — a shell literally named 'M.T. Lot,' *empty lot* — buying land at **$100 an acre**." The detail: "Stargate's first approvals came as a **'permit by rule'** — the same fast-track a **dry cleaner** or an **auto-body shop** gets — for a plant that could power a city." Authority (Kathryn Guerra): the industry "is expanding at a rate that is beyond the capability" of the Texas regulator to police. Rebuttal: "So this isn't Luddites panicking about robots. It's a consent problem."

## Both-sides weighing / calibration · pace:d · covers:[S2,S5,S1,S4] · serves-spine: steel-man hard per the guide, then land the calibrated verdict. LABEL the apocalypse framing as the overreach it is.
· **devices:** Steel-manning · qualification/hedging ("practically speaking," "to some degree") · contrast · the channel's both-sides verdict ("they're both right to some degree") · thought-experiment-style labeling of the overreach.
· **anchors:** Scale check: "nationally, all this water ≈ **5% of what US golf courses drink** — the country isn't running dry." "No blackouts — grids plan years ahead." "Some designs use basically no water; Meta even pipes **waste heat** into Danish homes." So the "whole-county-wiped-out" framing "is the overreach — and it hands Big Tech an easy villain." BUT the boosters dodge: "'water positive by 2030' mostly means **paying someone else** to save water, not using less"; and the **Jevons paradox** (1860s coal) — "make it cheaper to run and people just use way more." Verdict (match exemplar cadence): "Practically speaking, they're both right, to some degree. The panic's wrong about the scale; the boosters are wrong about the consent and the cost."

## Back third — human close (story/emotion-forward) · pace:d · covers:[S4,S1] · serves-spine: slow, personal; land the aphorism; adapted sign-off.
· **devices:** second-person/scene-setting · verbatim human quote · aphoristic close (exemplar cadence, not transplanted) · rhetorical reframe ("the question was never…").
· **anchors:** OG in Abilene VERBATIM: "We invest in our home, we serve our country, and we weren't even included in this process… I feel trapped." Donna VERBATIM-in-function: "she's just going to wave the white flag and move." Aphorism: "The question was never whether we build the future — we're building it either way. It's whether the people cashing in get to quietly hand the bill to the people who never got a vote — or whether we make them ask first." **Adapted sign-off** (neutral equivalent, no verbatim catchphrase / no members/Substack furniture): warm wrap + "if you got something out of this, like and subscribe" + "all my sources are in the description, go dig in" + "tell me where I'm wrong in the comments" + a plain "take care."
