# Candidate Angles — AI Data Center Debate

A thesis is a *debatable claim* the video must defend, not a topic summary.

---

**Angle 1 — The real AI data-center fight isn't "is AI good or bad" — it's *who pays and who decides*. A handful of trillion-dollar companies are quietly shifting the costs (your power bill, your water, your quiet, sometimes your home) onto local communities that never got a vote, while the profits and most of the tax breaks flow elsewhere.** *(reframe / hidden-detail pivot)*
- *supported by:* [S1] (bills +50%, ~$1B VA tax breaks, <150 jobs), [S2] (Lake Tahoe cutoff, eminent domain, rents doubling), [S4] (permit-by-rule with no public notice) · *tension:* Loudoun County's 38%-of-revenue windfall and property-tax cut [S5]; national water is only ~5% of golf courses [S2] — the boom genuinely delivers for some.
- *why compelling:* It sidesteps the exhausted "AI good/bad" shouting match and reframes the whole thing as an old, resonant American question — externalized costs and consent — with hard dollar figures on both sides.

**Angle 2 — Both the boosters and the doomers are wrong: the danger isn't the data centers, it's the *speed without guardrails* — we're sprinting into a decade-defining buildout with no public vote, no map, and regulators a decade behind.** *(contrarian / both-sides)*
- *supported by:* [S2] (explicitly anti-hysteria AND anti-recklessness), [S4] (regulators "beyond capacity," 10-year-old enforcement cases), [S1] (no official directory) · *tension:* someone has to move fast to "beat China," and grids do plan years ahead [S5].
- *why compelling:* Steelmans everyone and lands on process, not panic — very on-brand for the style's "they're probably both right" verdict.

**Angle 3 — The backup diesel generator — a bureaucratic afterthought — is the only reason we know these things exist. The secrecy is the story.** *(hidden-detail pivot)*
- *supported by:* [S1] (whole map built from air-quality permits; trade-secret redactions), [S2] (shell companies, Disney precedent), [S4] (NDAs, thermal drones) · *tension:* companies cite legitimate PR/competitor/security reasons for secrecy [S5].
- *why compelling:* A genuine "nobody's telling you this" reveal — but narrower, more about journalism than the core debate.

**Angle 4 — Efficiency won't save us. Every gain in AI chip efficiency is eaten by more demand (Jevons Paradox), so the "we'll go green by 2030" promises are a bet against 160 years of economics.** *(cause→effect)*
- *supported by:* [S4] (Jevons), [S1] (utilities reversing net-zero, buying coal/gas), [S3] (grid still fossil-heavy) · *tension:* real renewable/nuclear deals exist (Three Mile Island 2027), and closed-loop cooling is improving [S1,S5].
- *why compelling:* A crisp counterintuitive mechanism, but it's mostly the environmental slice, not the full debate.

**Angle 5 — We were promised "the cloud" — weightless, invisible, clean. What we got is the loudest, thirstiest factory in the neighborhood, and it's in someone's backyard right now.** *(comparison / reframe)*
- *supported by:* [S5] ("the cloud" = buildings), [S1] (noise, vibration, Carlos's kid), [S4] (thermal plumes) · *tension:* it IS the backbone of tools 900M people use [S4].
- *why compelling:* Strong imagery, but leans emotional; risks the "hysteria" trap S2 warns against.

---

**[CHOSEN] Angle 1 — "who pays and who decides."** It wins on both evidence and resonance: it's the only angle with hard numbers on *both* sides of the ledger (the +50% Virginia bills, ~$1B in tax breaks, and near-empty job counts against Loudoun's 38%-revenue windfall and the "5% of golf courses" reality check), so the video can steelman honestly instead of cheerleading. And "who bears the cost, who never got a vote" is a durable, bipartisan frame — exactly the fair-minded, both-sides posture the style guide is built around. Angles 2–5 all fold in as sections underneath it.
