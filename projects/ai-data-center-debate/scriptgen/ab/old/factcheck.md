# Fact-Check — AI Data Center Debate

One row per factual claim in `script.md`. `[S#]` = supported by a fetched source (quote given). `[needs-check]` = model knowledge / generalization, unverified.

## Ground Rules
- No official directory / no single regulator; companies hide ownership → **[S1]** "there is no definitive public directory of data centers, no official map, no single regulator." / trade-secret redactions.
- Council-meeting hysteria vs. real concerns; fearmongering discredits legitimate concerns → **[S2]** "fearmongering is the single fastest way to discredit legitimate concerns."
- CTA / "is AI good or evil" framing → rhetorical, no factual claim.

- New ones going up at more than two a week → **[S1]** "more than two every week."
- Amazon/Microsoft/Google/Meta own ~1/3 of capacity → **[S2]** "collectively own 1/3 of all data center capacity."
- 2025 capex Meta ~$64B, Google ~$75B, Microsoft ~$80B → **[S1]** "Meta planned to spend at least $64 billion… Google's 75 billion and Microsoft 80 billion."
- Stargate ~1,100 acres, bigger than Central Park → **[S4]** "1100 acres that's larger than Central Park."

## The Power Bill
- DOE: data-center electricity could reach ~12% of US total; ~4% in 2023 → **[S1]** "could soon require as much as 12% of total US electricity use. In 2023, it was just over 4%." (Corroborated by **[S5]** 4.4% in 2023, 7–12% by 2028.)
- IEA: one large data center ≈ electricity of 400,000 electric cars → **[S3]** "one large Data Center uses the same amount of electricity as it takes to power 400,000 electric cars."
- Much of what's built now runs on natural gas → **[S4]** "the vast majority are powered by fossil fuels" / "80 gigawatts of natural gas power under construction." National generalization beyond TX/NE → **[needs-check]** (softened to "a lot of what's actually getting built").
- Coal that was supposed to be retired → **[S1]** utility "voted to postpone closing down two coal fired power plants."
- Nebraska: postponed two coal plants, approved two new gas plants for data-center demand → **[S1]** "decided to build two new natural gas plants. All to meet spikes in electricity demand, mainly driven by data centers."
- Virginia Dominion: double generation by 2039, up to $103B, bills up as much as 50% → **[S1]** "roughly double its electricity generation by 2039… could cost up to $103 billion, increasing residential electricity bills by as much as 50%."

- Carlos, Manassas VA, ~$20,000 on windows/insulation vs. hum of 3 Amazon data centers ~200m away → **[S1]** "spent $20,000 on insulation and replacing all the upstairs windows" / "Three Amazon data centers sit roughly 200 m away."
- His son had nightmares → **[S1]** "Carlos's son started having nightmares."
- Amazon says centers run below required sound levels → **[S1]** "An Amazon spokesperson told us the centers operate well below required sound levels."
- Noise rules never written for 24/7 drone → **[S1]** "never designed with the 24/7 drone of modern data centers in mind."

## The Jobs Myth
- Largest data centers employ fewer than 150 permanent workers; some as few as 25 → **[S1]** "even the largest data centers employ fewer than 150 permanent workers and some have as few as 25."
- Stargate Abilene ~100 full-time; cheese plant same footprint projected 500 → **[S2]** "about 100 full-time employees" / "cheese packaging plant… projected to employ 500 people."
- 37 states offer tax incentives → **[S1]** "We tracked 37 states offering tax incentive programs."
- Virginia: 56 projects, ~$1B tax savings in one year → **[S1]** "56 data center projects received tax savings of almost a billion dollars in the 2023 fiscal year."
- Louisiana parish rents ~$650 → ~$2,000 after Meta → **[S2]** "a rental that might have been 6 700 a month is now 2,500" / "rents have increased from $650 a month to 2,000."
- Georgia/Kentucky eminent-domain threats for power lines → **[S2]** "It is being taken by force by Georgia Power… It is called eminent domain" / "more than a dozen farmers in Kentucky recently received threats that eminent domain will be used."

## Both Sides
- Loudoun County: data centers ~38% of general-fund revenue; county cut property taxes → **[S5]** "data centers generate about 38% of their general funds revenue… they've actually been able to cut property taxes."
- Closed-loop cooling can push water use close to zero → **[S5]** "when operators can use fully closed loop systems, they can aim for water use effectiveness as close to zero as possible."
- Nationally, data-center water use a small fraction of golf-course use → **[S2]** "water withdrawals… account for about 5% of the water usage of golf courses."
- Jevons paradox (1860s): efficiency grows demand; AI efficiency gains erased by usage → **[S4]** "Jevons Paradox. It was first described in the 1860s… efficiency wouldn't save coal because cheaper, more efficient power would lead to an overall rise in demand."

## Close
