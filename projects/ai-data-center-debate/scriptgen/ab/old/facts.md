# Fact Sheet — AI Data Center Debate

Through-line: *The AI data-center boom is real and unstoppable — the honest debate isn't "AI good vs. evil," it's who pays the costs (power, water, quality of life) versus who gets the benefits (tax breaks, capacity, profit), and whether those are the same people.*

## Beat 1 — Hook (Lake Tahoe)
- Lake Tahoe is home to some of the richest people in California; median property values north of $700,000. `[S2]`
- ~50,000 people on the California side (Fortune says 49,000 residents) face losing their power source after ~May 2027. `[S2]`
- NV Energy (Nevada utility) told Liberty Utilities (small CA utility) it will stop supplying power after May 2027 because it needs the capacity for data centers. `[S2]`
- Buying replacement power on the wholesale market could cost 2–3x, minimum. `[S2]`
- Resident Danielle Hughes: "It's like we don't exist." `[S2]`
- A single large data center can consume more power than half a million homes. `[S2]`

## Beat 2 — Ground rules / framing
- There is no official public directory of data centers, no single map, no single regulator; companies use trade-secret exemptions and NDAs to hide details. `[S1]`
- Fearmongering (whole-county-wiped-out rhetoric) is counterproductive and discredits legitimate concerns; real concerns coexist with hysteria. `[S2]`

## Beat 3 — The Scale
- At least 1,240 data centers built or approved in the US by end of 2024 — nearly 4x the number in 2010. `[S1]`
- New data centers going up at more than two per week. `[S1]`
- Amazon/Microsoft/Google/Meta collectively own ~1/3 of all data-center capacity. `[S2]`
- 177 of the pinpointed data centers belong to Amazon. `[S1]`
- 2025 capex: Meta ~$64B, Google ~$75B, Microsoft ~$80B. `[S1]`
- Projected global data-center infrastructure investment ~$7 trillion by 2030. `[S4]`
- Stargate (Texas): ~4M sq ft across ~1,100 acres — larger than Central Park. `[S4]`

## Beat 4 — The Power Bill
- DOE report: data-center electricity use could reach ~12% of total US electricity; in 2023 it was just over 4%. `[S1]` (S5: 4.4% in 2023, 7–12% by 2028.)
- IEA: one large data center uses about as much electricity as 400,000 electric cars. `[S3]`
- Companies announce renewables/nuclear, but much of what's being built now runs on natural gas (Texas: ~80 GW gas under construction, ~half exclusively for data centers off-grid). `[S4]`
- National framing that "a lot of what's built right now is gas/coal" beyond TX + NE. `[needs-check]` (soften in script)
- Nebraska: utility postponed closing two coal plants and approved two new gas plants, largely for data-center demand. `[S1]`
- Virginia: Dominion Energy said it must roughly double generation by 2039, costing up to $103 billion, and residential bills could rise as much as 50%. `[S1]`

## Beat 5 — Water & the Neighbors
- Up to 43% of the largest data centers sit in areas of high/extremely high water stress; they use drinking water to cool servers. `[S1]`
- Microsoft planned ~1 million gallons/day per building in Arizona, ~1.83 billion gallons/year — ~a city the size of Santa Cruz / ~61,000 Americans. `[S1]`
- Carlos Janis (Manassas, VA) spent ~$20,000 on windows/insulation to escape the hum of three Amazon data centers ~200 m away; his 7-year-old had nightmares. `[S1]`
- Amazon says its centers operate well below required sound levels. `[S1]`
- The characterization that noise rules "were never written for a 24/7 industrial drone next to homes." `[S1]` (source: "never designed with the 24/7 drone of modern data centers in mind")
- ABC News flew thermal drones over Texas data centers and found measurable pollution plumes; fine particulate matter linked to asthma, heart attacks, premature death; homeowners often not told the plant was coming. `[S4]`

## Beat 6 — The Jobs Myth / economics
- Even the largest data centers employ fewer than 150 permanent workers; some as few as 25. `[S1]`
- Stargate (Abilene, TX): ~100 full-time employees once complete; a same-footprint cheese-packaging plant was projected to employ 500. `[S2]`
- 37 states offer tax-incentive programs to data centers. `[S1]`
- Virginia: 56 data-center projects received ~$1 billion in tax savings in FY2023. `[S1]`
- Richland Parish, Louisiana: rents rose from ~$650 to ~$2,000–$2,500/month after Meta's ~$27B data center arrived. `[S2]`
- Eminent domain used/threatened for transmission lines: Georgia Power (Coweta County), Kentucky farmers, Dominion (Charlotte County farmer Todd Lax, ~$1,500/acre offer). `[S2]`

## Beat 7 — Both-sides weighing / verdict
- Loudoun County, VA (world's largest data-center hub): data centers generate ~38% of general-fund revenue — enough that the county cut property taxes. `[S5]`
- Fully closed-loop cooling can push water-use effectiveness close to zero. `[S5]`
- National data-center water withdrawals are roughly 5% of what golf courses use. `[S2]`
- Jevons paradox (first described in the 1860s by W.S. Jevons): efficiency gains grow total demand rather than shrink it; AI efficiency gains are being erased by usage growth. `[S4]`
- Renewables won't power AI at real scale before ~2028; new nuclear is a decade or more away. `[S4]`

## Beat 8 — Close
- Behind-the-meter/off-grid power can reduce grid strain and may keep consumer prices down, but means more plants emitting pollutants. `[S4]`
- Best practice: power-first siting, transparency on water, community integration. `[S5]`
- "Next industrial revolution" framing of the buildout. `[S5]`
- No national referendum/vote has been held on the buildout; hundreds of centers under construction now. `[S2]`
