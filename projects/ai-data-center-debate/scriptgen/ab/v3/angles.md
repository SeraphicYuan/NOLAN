# Angles — AI Data Center Debate (v3)

## Inferred spine type (from `channel-stickman-talks/style_guide.md`)

**This channel's spine is a fair-minded, data-grounded WEIGHING of one genuinely two-sided
question — a "how worried should we actually be?" explainer/threat built on CONTRAST.** It is
not a mystery, not a biography, not a mechanism tour, and not a one-viewpoint hot take. The
guide's own workhorse device is *contrast/juxtaposition* (promise vs. reality, credentials vs.
polling, preference vs. likelihood); its universal move is *steel-manning both sides and then
rendering a hedged verdict* ("they're probably both right to some degree"); and its "explainer/
threat" format runs **alarm → context → evidence timeline → thought-experiment → both-sides
verdict.** The narrator's whole promise is "trust me because I'm fair and I show my work,"
separating *what I'd personally prefer* from *what's actually likely/true.* So the right angle
here is a **felt contrast that a fair adjudicator walks you through** — never an argue-it-out
lecture.

## Candidate angles (all of the inferred type)

### Angle 1 — "The cloud has a zip code — and the people who live at it never got a vote." **[CHOSEN]**
- *core:* the CONTRAST between AI's invisible, national, futuristic *promise* and its very
  visible, local, present-tense *cost* — a 75-ft humming box in a 30-year resident's backyard.
  The honest verdict: the tech is real and mostly here to stay; the scandal is *who pays and who
  decided.*
- *supported by:* [S1] (Donna, Carlos, water, jobs, tax breaks, grid bills), [S4] (Stargate, OG,
  permits), [S2] (Lake Tahoe, eminent domain — as grounding), [S5]/[S3] (what it is, both-sides).
- *why a viewer cares:* everyone types into a chatbot; almost nobody pictures the neighbor who
  can't sleep because of it. It reframes an abstract tech-story as *your* fairness question.
- **Resonance 5 · Evidence 5 · Style-fit 5**

### Angle 2 — "How worried should you actually be about AI data centers?"
- *core:* the channel's literal explainer/threat format — sort the *real* concerns (local power,
  water, eminent domain, un-permitted turbines) from the *hysteria* (poisoned counties,
  surveillance psyops).
- *supported by:* [S2], [S4], [S5], [S1].
- *why a viewer cares:* it answers the exact anxious question the algorithm serves them.
- **Resonance 4 · Evidence 5 · Style-fit 5** — strong, but risks becoming a myth-busting checklist
  without a single felt spine; folded *into* Angle 1 as its steel-man turn.

### Angle 3 — "We're mid-sprint into the biggest build-out since the railroads, and nobody signed the terms."
- *core:* national-race-vs-no-public-vote contrast; Jevons Paradox as the turn (efficiency won't
  save us).
- *supported by:* [S4] (Jevons, $800B/$1T), [S5], [S1].
- *why a viewer cares:* the scale is genuinely staggering.
- **Resonance 4 · Evidence 4 · Style-fit 4** — good, but "scale" is colder than "your neighbor";
  its best beats (Jevons, the no-vote frame) live inside Angle 1.

### Angle 4 — "What's actually inside the box, and why AI made it so thirsty." (mechanism explainer)
- *core:* how a data center works; GPUs, cooling, water, power.
- *supported by:* [S3], [S5].
- *why a viewer cares:* mild curiosity.
- **Resonance 2 · Evidence 5 · Style-fit 2** — *Penalized.* This is a mechanism tour, a spine
  type THIS guide does not build on. Grounding material, not the spine.

### Angle 5 — "Both sides are right — the fight was never AI-or-no-AI." (pure both-sides)
- *core:* steel-man builders and neighbors, conclude both have a point.
- *supported by:* all sources.
- *why a viewer cares:* it's the channel's signature verdict.
- **Resonance 3 · Evidence 5 · Style-fit 4** — *Penalized mildly:* it's the *verdict*, not a felt
  through-line; without a concrete human spine it drifts toward an isolated analysis beat. Used as
  Angle 1's close.

## Winner

**Angle 1 — "The cloud has a zip code, and the people who live at it never got a vote."**
It wins on **resonance + style-fit**: it turns on the exact contrast the guide is built to walk
you through (invisible national promise vs. concrete local bill), it's carried by named, humanized
people the audience can feel, and it lands naturally on the channel's hedged both-sides verdict —
*the tech is real, the neighbors are also right, and the real scandal is who pays and who decided* —
without ever needing an isolated lecture beat. Angles 2, 3, and 5 are absorbed as its steel-man
turn, its scale-context, and its close; Angle 4 is demoted to grounding.
