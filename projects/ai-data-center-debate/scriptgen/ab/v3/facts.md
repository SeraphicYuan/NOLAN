## EVIDENCE — the local cost (the heart; accelerated fact-clusters)

### Scale / concentration

- Loudoun County, VA = "data center alley"; as much as a third of the planet's internet traffic flows through Virginia; the 329 data centers tracked consumed almost a quarter of Virginia's electricity in 2023. — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Combined, the 1,200+ tracked centers could soon consume more power than all of Poland did in 2023; AI could drive up to 600 TWh by 2028. — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports

## TURN — steel-man both sides / separate real from hysterical (decelerate)

- Jevons Paradox: efficiency won't save us. When 1860s coal plants got more efficient, W.S. Jevons showed demand rose and total coal use *grew*; AI chips are getting more efficient, but overall AI growth is outrunning the gains. — src:[S4] · purpose:authority · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports
- Steel-man the calm side (from the exposé source itself): nationally, all data-center water withdrawals ≈ ~5% of what US golf courses use; apocalyptic "the whole county will be wiped out, the air is poison" framing is counterproductive and discredits the real concerns. — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:claimed · role:challenges
- Myth-check (neutral explainer): data centers don't cause blackouts (grids plan years ahead); diesel stacks are backup-only; not all are water guzzlers (designs range from air-cooled to closed-loop). — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:challenges
- Real upside exists: Loudoun County's budget says data centers generate ~38% of its general-fund revenue, letting it *cut* property taxes. — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:challenges
- The tech is genuinely useful and mostly here to stay; construction jobs are real (if temporary); some closed-loop designs barely use water. — src:[S5,S3] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:challenges
- Historical precedent for over-panic: early-20th-c. power-line hysteria; anti-fracking "flaming tap water." Some fear always attends new tech — the real question is what the *actual* problem is. — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:claimed · role:challenges
