# Beat-map — AI Data Center Debate (v3)

Angle: **"The cloud has a zip code, and the people who live at it never got a vote."**
Spine type: fair-minded, data-grounded WEIGHING of a two-sided question (explainer/threat), built
on the promise-vs-local-cost CONTRAST.

Constitution constraints applied (`channel-stickman-talks`):
- **Hook:** open with the source's genuine shock (Lake Tahoe) as a cold-open, then pivot within
  two sentences to the work + spine — matches Hook Pattern #1 ("We need to talk about ___" +
  immediate hard fact) and #3 (time-sensitive stakes).
- **Structure:** Hook → intro/ground-rules + soft CTA → segmented body (explainer/threat arc:
  alarm → context → evidence timeline → thought-experiment) → both-sides weighing → sign-off.
- **Braided, not chaptered.** No numbered signposting, no isolated analysis-only section. Every
  fact cashed out against the angle.
- **Pacing:** accelerate on fact-clusters (scale, water, money); decelerate for the humanized
  bedroom-noise beats, the Jevons/both-sides turn, and the close.
- **Non-negotiables:** data-ground every claim; steel-man the calm side fairly; hedge honestly
  ("on paper," "practically speaking," "to some degree"); label the thought-experiment.
- Counter-source (S2's exposé/eminent-domain material) deployed as the HOOK + an accelerated
  fact-cluster, NEVER as an argue-it-out lecture.

## Hook · pace:a · covers:[S2,S1] · serves-spine: Lake Tahoe cold-open — 49,000 people about to lose their power *to* data centers — makes the invisible cloud suddenly land on real homes; pivot in two sentences to "what these buildings are and who's paying for them."

## Intro / ground-rules + soft CTA · pace:d · covers:[S5,S2] · serves-spine: Restate the question ("how worried should we actually be?"), set the fair-adjudicator posture (I'm not here to fear-monger or cheerlead; separate the real from the hysterical), light like/subscribe nod. Establishes trust so the contrast reads as evidence-led.

## Context — what the box actually is · pace:a · covers:[S5,S3] · serves-spine: "The cloud isn't in the sky — it's a warehouse." GPUs vs CPUs, ChatGPT ~10x a search, IEA 400,000-EV figure, 4.4%→7–12% of US power. Grounds the promise side: this is a genuine revolution, trillions flowing (Stargate, $800B→$1T). Makes the later cost land harder.

## Evidence 1 — the scale, at your address · pace:a · covers:[S1] · serves-spine: 1,240 centers (4x since 2010), "two a week," data-center alley = a quarter of Virginia's power, could out-consume Poland. Zooms the abstract number down onto a map with neighborhoods on it.

## Evidence 2 — the bedroom next door (humanize) · pace:d · covers:[S1,S4] · serves-spine: Donna's headphones-in-the-basement; Carlos's $20k windows and his son's "spaceship"; OG's "I feel trapped." Amazon's "well below required levels" rebuttal included. This IS the spine — the felt local cost.

## Evidence 3 — water, money, and who gets the bill · pace:a · covers:[S1,S4,S5] · serves-spine: 43% in water stress, Microsoft AZ 1.83B gal/yr, Council Bluffs ~1B gal; Dominion's up-to-$103B / +50% bills; ~100 permanent jobs vs a 500-job cheese plant; $1B in VA tax breaks; Meta's "Sidecat"/Google's "Magellan" shell games; eminent domain (Ansley, Kentucky farmers, Lax). Cluster proving: benefit floats off, cost stays local.

## Turn — steel-man the calm side (thought-experiment + both-sides) · pace:d · covers:[S5,S2,S4] · serves-spine: Fairness demands the other case: myth-check (no blackouts, backup-only diesel, closed-loop designs), national water ≈ 5% of golf courses, Loudoun's 38% revenue + tax cuts, power-line/fracking panics. Then the honest counter-weight: Jevons Paradox (efficiency won't save us) + un-permitted turbines (Colossus) + "permit by rule = autobody shop." Verdict: both sides are partly right.

## Close — who pays and who decided · pace:d · covers:[S2,S1,S4] · serves-spine: The real question isn't AI-or-no-AI; the tech is here to stay. It's that "there have been no national referendums on any of this," and the people paying never got a vote. Land on Donna's white flag / OG's "we weren't included." Aphoristic close.
