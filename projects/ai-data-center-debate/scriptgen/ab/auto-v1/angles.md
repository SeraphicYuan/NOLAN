# Candidate angles — AI Data Center Debate

**Inferred spine type (from the style guide):** a *data-grounded, both-sides argument* — pose one sharp question, pin every claim to a number/date/dollar figure, steel-man each side fairly, explicitly separate "what I'd personally prefer" from "what's actually likely/fair," hedge honestly, and land a calibrated verdict (often "they're both right to some degree"). NOT a human-thematic braid and NOT a dry how-it-works explainer — the mechanism (S3/S5) is *grounding*, and the loudest activism (S2 clip) is a *hook*, not the thesis.

**The trap to avoid:** this channel's #1 DON'T is "don't preach a single viewpoint or perform outrage." So a one-sided "data centers are evil" screed is off-type, even though the harm footage is vivid. The right angle has to hold both sides on real numbers and reach a fair verdict.

---

### Angle 1 — The fight isn't AI vs. no-AI; it's *who pays* — and right now the bill lands on the people with the least say  **[CHOSEN]**
- *thesis:* The AI buildout is real and basically unstoppable. The actual, winnable argument is about cost allocation — the benefits are diffuse (all of us use AI; Big Tech profits; a few counties get tax revenue) while the costs are hyper-concentrated (noise, water, higher power bills, seized land, and almost no permanent jobs) on specific host communities that mostly didn't get a vote.
- *core (the contrast):* diffuse benefit vs. concentrated cost — and the transparency gap (NDAs, shell LLCs, a "permit by rule" you'd give a dry cleaner) that lets it happen quietly.
- *supported by:* [S1] map + bills + jobs + water · [S2] Lake Tahoe + eminent domain + shell companies · [S4] Stargate + permit-by-rule · [S5] Loudoun's 38% (the pro side) · [S3] scale.
- *why a viewer cares:* it's their electric bill, their home value, and whether anyone asked them — the most personal possible framing of a trillion-dollar abstraction.
- **Resonance 5/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 5/5**
- *Why it wins:* it IS the channel's signature move — separate preference from what's-actually-likely, steel-man both sides, follow the money, land a fair verdict — and it converts the loudest hysteria (Angle 3) into the cold-open hook while absorbing Angles 2, 4, and 5 as body segments. Deeply resonant without preaching.

### Angle 2 — Nobody voted on this: the biggest buildout of our lifetime is happening with less public input than a new dry cleaner
- *thesis:* The core scandal is procedural — secrecy, shell companies, and fast-track permits, not the technology itself.
- *core:* transparency/consent as the fault line (NDAs, redacted trade-secret records, permit-by-rule).
- *supported by:* [S1] no public directory · [S2] shell LLCs · [S4] permit-by-rule + overwhelmed regulators.
- *why a viewer cares:* consent-of-the-governed is a shared-across-the-aisle principle.
- **Resonance 4/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 5/5**
- *Note:* excellent and perfectly on-type, but narrower — it's really the *turn* inside Angle 1. Folded in as the sharpest body segment, not the whole spine.

### Angle 3 — The hysteria is wrong, and that's exactly what Big Tech is counting on
- *thesis:* Apocalyptic fear-mongering (poisoned air, whole counties wiped out) discredits the real, narrower problems and plays into Big Tech's hands.
- *core:* separate legitimate concern from panic (national water = ~5% of golf courses; no blackouts; myths debunked).
- *supported by:* [S2] anti-hysteria argument · [S5] myth-busting.
- *why a viewer cares:* it flatters the viewer's wish to be the reasonable one in the room.
- **Resonance 4/5** · **Evidence 4/5** · **Style-fit 5/5** (this is literally the channel's steel-man posture)
- *Penalized as a spine:* as the thesis it becomes a media-criticism/meta beat and risks reading as dismissive of genuinely harmed residents. Best used as the HOOK + the calibration beat — the "counter-source as hook, not thesis" the task calls for.

### Angle 4 — Green promises, gas reality: the AI sold as a climate fix is running on some of the dirtiest power we've got
- *thesis:* Renewable/nuclear pledges vs. what's actually being built — natural gas, coal plants kept open, diesel/turbines, Jevons paradox.
- *core:* promise vs. reality (a classic channel micro-arc).
- *supported by:* [S4] gas buildout + Jevons · [S1] utilities reversing net-zero · [S3] grid still fossil-heavy.
- **Resonance 3/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 4/5**
- *Note:* strong data but single-issue (environment) and closest to the "preach one viewpoint" trap. Used as one body segment inside Angle 1, not the spine.

### Angle 5 — Can the grid take it — and who picks up a $100 billion tab?
- *thesis:* Grid strain + cost pass-through to ordinary ratepayers is the sleeper story.
- *core:* who pays for the upgrades (Dominion's $103B / +50% bills; Lake Tahoe priced out).
- *supported by:* [S1] Dominion · [S2] Lake Tahoe · [S5] PJM +30 GW by 2030.
- **Resonance 4/5** · **Evidence 5/5** · **Style-fit 5/5**
- *Note:* the "your bill" half of Angle 1. Folded in as a body segment.

---

**Winner: Angle 1.** It wins on **resonance** (it reframes a trillion-dollar abstraction as your bill, your home, and whether anyone asked you) and on **style-fit** (it is the channel's exact both-sides / follow-the-money / preference-vs-likelihood machine). It uses the loudest activism as the hook, steel-mans the genuine pro-build case (Loudoun's revenue, the myth-busting, waste-heat reuse), and lands a calibrated verdict instead of a sermon.
