# Beat-map — AI Data Center Debate  (constitution = channel-stickman-talks/style_guide.md)

**Angle (spine):** The fight isn't AI vs. no-AI — it's *who pays*. Benefits are diffuse (we all use AI; Big Tech profits; a few counties bank tax revenue); costs are concentrated on host communities that mostly didn't get a vote (noise, water, higher bills, seized land, almost no permanent jobs) — and a transparency gap lets it happen quietly. Contrast = diffuse benefit vs. concentrated cost.

**Retention rules imported from THIS guide (HARD):** hook = why-this-matters-now + immediately a hard fact/quote/clip; intro block restates topic, sets ground rules (separate preference from likelihood), soft like/subscribe; sponsor read *thematically fused* with a coded URL, bracketed cleanly; segmented body, each segment a micro-arc (setup → data → verdict → transition); **signpost every transition with a spoken cue** ("Now," "Here's the thing," "Let's start with," "Moving on though," "Now let's zoom out") and recap-then-tease at seams; DATA-GROUND everything; STEEL-MAN the other side before judging; HEDGE ("on paper," "practically speaking," "to some degree"); LABEL speculation as a thought experiment; describe outrage, don't perform it; standard sign-off. NOTE: this channel's signposting is spoken cues, NOT numbered "First/Second/In conclusion" — the task's "no numbered signposting" DON'T = avoid numbered lists, but DO use the channel's spoken transitions. Back third stays story/emotion-forward. Target ~1200 words.

## Hook · pace:d · covers:[S2,S1] · serves-spine: "We need to talk about ___" urgency + the loudest clip. Open on the councilwoman's apocalyptic warning, then within two sentences reframe: she's mostly right about the *concern* and mostly wrong about the *scale*, and that gap is the whole video. Ground instantly in a hard fact (two new data centers a week; by some estimates one can outdraw half a million homes). Deploys the controversy AS the hook, not the thesis.

Self-deprecation ("I'm just a stickman"), bipartisan invite. Soft like/subscribe.

## Segment A — "The boom is real" (steel-man the pro side) · pace:a · covers:[S5,S4,S3,S1] · serves-spine: Establish scale so the piece isn't anti-tech: next-industrial-revolution money ($800B in 2026 → $1T+ in 2027 ≈ US defense budget; ~$7T by 2030), real demand (chatbot users 400M→900M), the power reality (4.4% of US electricity in 2023 → 7–12% by 2028; a prompt ~10× a search), and the genuine local upside (Loudoun's 38% of budget → property-tax cuts). Verdict: we are not un-building this. Tease the catch.

## Segment B — "So where does the bill land?" (the concentrated costs) · pace:a→d · covers:[S1,S2,S4] · serves-spine: The heart. Rapid, data-grounded roll of concentrated costs, each cashed against "who pays": your power bill (Dominion +50%, up to $103B), priced out entirely (Lake Tahoe, ~49k, May 2027), water in the desert (43% in high stress; Arizona 1.83B gal ≈ 61k people), the noise you can feel (Carlos, $20k, the "spaceship" nightmare — decelerate here for the human beat), seized land (Georgia/Kentucky/Virginia eminent domain), and the kicker — almost no permanent jobs (<150, some 25; Stargate ~100). Verdict: diffuse benefit, concentrated cost.

## Segment C — "And nobody really voted on it" (the turn) · pace:a · covers:[S1,S2,S4] · serves-spine: The sharpest, most resonant beat — consent. No public directory; ownership hidden behind shell LLCs (Google's "Jetstream," Meta's "$60M Sicat" deal); and the killer: Stargate got a "permit by rule," the same fast-track a dry cleaner gets, for a plant that could power a city — plus a former regulator saying the industry has outrun the agency. Verdict: this is a process problem, not a Luddite panic.

## Both-sides weighing / calibration · pace:d · covers:[S2,S5,S1,S4] · serves-spine: Steel-man hard, per the guide. National water = ~5% of golf courses; no blackouts; some designs are water-free; Meta reuses waste heat in Denmark — so the apocalypse framing IS overblown. BUT the pledges lean on offsets, and Jevons paradox eats the efficiency gains. Land the channel's verdict: "they're probably both right to some degree" — the panic is wrong about scale, the boosters are wrong about consent and cost. Label the scary version explicitly as the overreach it is.

## Back third — human close (story/emotion-forward) · pace:d · covers:[S4,S1,S2] · serves-spine: Slow, personal. Return to OG in Abilene ("we serve our country and we weren't even included… I feel trapped") and Donna waving the white flag. Aphoristic close in the channel's register: the real question was never whether we build the future, it's whether the people who benefit get to hand the bill to the people who never got a vote — and whether we make them ask first. Then the standard sign-off (like/sub, sources in description + newsletter, thank members, "all in good fun," "take care").
