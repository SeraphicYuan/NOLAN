## Beat: CONTEXT  (the boom is real and probably unstoppable — steel-man the pro side)

- The buildout is framed as "the next industrial revolution"; OpenAI/Oracle/SoftBank's "Stargate" aims to invest "trillions with a T"; Nvidia pledged $100B toward OpenAI — src:[S5] · purpose:authority · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- Big tech is projected to spend more than $800 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026, exceeding $1 trillion in 2027 — "on par with the entire US defense budget" — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- By 2030 companies are estimated to invest nearly $7 trillion in data-center infrastructure in the AI race — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- 2025 capex plans: Meta at least $64B on facilities/equipment, Google $75B, Microsoft $80B — src:[S1,S5] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- Data centers used ~4.4% of total US electricity in 2023 (DOE); could reach 7–12% by 2028 — src:[S5] (a 2024 DOE report cited in [S1] says "as much as 12%") · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- A single generative-AI query draws far more power than a search: ~2.9 Wh for a ChatGPT prompt vs ~0.3 Wh for a Google search (roughly 10×); IEA puts it near 10× — src:[S5,S3] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- One large data center uses about the same electricity as it takes to power 400,000 electric cars (IEA) — src:[S3] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- The AI race is driven by real demand: OpenAI reported global chatbot use "more than doubled in the last year, going from 400 to 900 million users" — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- Some host communities genuinely profit: Loudoun County, VA's budget says data centers generate ~38% of general-fund revenue — enough that the county *cut* property taxes — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:context · conf:verified · role:challenges
- The build phase creates lots of jobs (data centers can employ 1,000+ people for the months/years of construction) — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:context · conf:verified · role:challenges

## Beat: EVIDENCE  (the bill lands somewhere — the concentrated local costs)

- The most comprehensive tally to date mapped more than 1,200 data centers built or approved by end of 2024 — nearly four times the number in 2010 — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Northern Virginia is "data center alley": as much as a third of the planet's internet traffic flows through Virginia; 329 tracked data centers consumed almost a quarter of the state's electricity in 2023 — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Noise/health:** residents near Amazon/Google sites report a 24/7 drone they can "feel"; Carlos Janis spent ~$20,000 on insulation and windows and still can't sleep; his 7-year-old had nightmares of "a spaceship outside" — src:[S1] · purpose:humanize · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- The American Public Health Association says chronic noise exposure can lead to cardiovascular disease and increased stress — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Water:** up to 43% of the largest data centers sit in areas of high or extremely high water stress — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- A Microsoft Arizona campus planned to use 1 million gallons of water a day per building — 1.83 billion gallons a year, "enough water for roughly 61,000 Americans" — in a state gripped by drought — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Grid & your bill:** Virginia's Dominion Energy said it must roughly double generation by 2039 (mainly for data centers + EVs), an expansion that could cost up to $103 billion and raise residential bills by as much as 50% — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Priced out:** NV Energy told Lake Tahoe's small utility it will stop supplying power after May 2027 because it needs the capacity for data centers, leaving ~49,000 residents scrambling; California regulators can't order a Nevada utility to serve them — src:[S2] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Few permanent jobs:** even the largest data centers employ fewer than 150 permanent workers — some as few as 25; Abilene's Stargate is projected at ~100 full-time once built — src:[S1,S2] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Dirty power reality:** most of what's actually being built now runs on natural gas; renewables at scale aren't expected before 2028 and new nuclear is a decade or more away — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Stargate (rural Texas) already has 62 diesel generators and 10 gas turbines with plans for 41 more; its onsite plant could top 1.7 GW (>1 million homes) and emit >7.8 million tons of greenhouse gas a year — ~2 million cars' worth — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- In Texas, ~80 GW of new natural gas is under construction and roughly half will power data centers directly, "behind the meter," off the public grid — src:[S4] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Eminent domain:** in Coweta County, Georgia a resident's childhood home is "being taken by force by Georgia Power" for a data-center transmission line; more than a dozen Kentucky farmers got eminent-domain threats (the Guardian); a Virginia farmer says Dominion offered ~$1,500/acre for his land — src:[S2] · purpose:humanize · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports

## Beat: TURN  (the sharp one — nobody voted on this / transparency)

- There is "no official record of how many are being built, where they are, or even who owns them"; BI met "so much resistance for records" they had to reverse-engineer a map from backup-generator air permits — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports
- Ownership is routinely hidden behind shell LLCs: a Google data center in Ohio was permitted under "Magellan Enterprises LLC"; Google negotiated land through a Delaware shell called "Jetstream LLC" in at least five cities (Washington Post); Meta signed a ~$60 million, 15-year Ohio tax deal as "Sicat LLC" before anyone knew it was Facebook's parent — src:[S1,S2] · purpose:proof · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports
- **The killer detail:** in Texas, Stargate's initial approvals came in as a "permit by rule" — the same low-level, no-public-notice authorization an auto-body shop or a dry cleaner gets — for a facility that could power hundreds of thousands of homes — src:[S4] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports
- A former Texas environmental regulator: "The data center industry is expanding at a rate that is beyond the capability of the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality to sufficiently regulate" — src:[S4] · purpose:authority · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports
- 37 states offer tax-incentive programs to data centers; Virginia handed 56 projects nearly $1 billion in tax savings in fiscal 2023 alone — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports

## Beat: CLOSE / both-sides calibration + human stakes

- **Steel-man (scale check):** nationally, all data-center water withdrawals combined are only about 5% of what US golf courses use — the water crisis is *local*, not national — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges
- **Steel-man (myths):** designs range from water-free air cooling to closed-loop systems; grids plan years ahead, so data centers don't cause blackouts; diesel stacks are emergency backup, not all-day runners — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges
- **Steel-man (it can be done well):** Meta's Denmark data center exports 100–165 GWh of waste heat a year to a district-heating system — proof the downsides aren't inevitable — src:[S5] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges
- **But the honest limit:** big-tech "water positive by 2030" and carbon pledges rely on offsets/credits — paying others to save water or supply green power — not on the local site using less — src:[S1] · purpose:proof · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- **Jevons paradox:** chips get more efficient, but (as economist William Stanley Jevons showed with coal in the 1860s) efficiency gains get erased by rising demand — src:[S4] · purpose:authority · beat:close · conf:verified · role:context
- **The human close:** in Abilene, TX, Omaira "OG" Garcia — a veteran who found her "own little piece of happiness" — says of Stargate next door: "We invest in our home, we serve our country, and we weren't even included in this process… I feel trapped" — src:[S4] · purpose:humanize · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- Donna Gallant, 30 years in Prince William County, VA, on the data centers surrounding her: "I can only fight for so long… then I'm going to wave the white flag and I'm going to pack up and I'm going to leave" — src:[S1] · purpose:humanize · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- The skeptic's own bottom line: "Does that mean we should never build any data centers… abandon AI entirely? Of course not" — but don't race "with no guardrails… based on some vague notion that we need to beat China" — src:[S2] · purpose:authority · beat:close · conf:verified · role:context
