# Voice pass — draft-01 vs. `art-stories` style guide

This guide labels its rhetorical toolkit **[all consistent across the corpus]** — treated here as the
`[universal]`-equivalent floor. Sentence-level moves are `[consistent]`. Checked by ear against the draft.

## Devices — where each lands

| Device (consistent = universal floor) | Where it lands in the draft | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Contrast / juxtaposition** (the engine) | Hook (equalizer vs. the one cut that "breaks the pattern"); the whole Procession→Reveal wall (skewers an abbot / helps the ploughman); "executioner … thief … gentle field-hand" | Landed, load-bearing |
| **Rhetorical questions** | Reveal: "So which is he? The executioner of the abbot, or the tired laborer's last helping hand?" · Close: "mercy, or a verdict?" | Landed |
| **Visual deixis** (verbal pointing) | Surface analysis: "Watch his hands," "watch what breaks" · Reveal: "look at what he is doing" | Landed |
| **Appeal to authority** (dates, names, quotes, institutions) | Editions counts; 1538 Lyon / Trechsel; "most marvelous woodcuts ever made"; "one scholar's phrase"; "one account" | Landed |
| **Visual imagery** (concrete, tactile) | "a worn-out team of horses across a stubborn field"; "Crowns snap. Swords shatter."; "grinning skeleton" | Landed |
| **Contextual reframing** (re-label the work) | Recontextualization: "The book is not really saying 'you must die.' … the way Death comes for you is a kind of verdict." | Landed — the climax move |
| **Personification** | Death as "field-hand," "usher," "thief," "a force" | Landed |
| **Allusion** (esp. Miltonic — guide notes it recurs *in the devil-themed scripts*) | Not a devil subject → **deliberately not forced.** A Reformation allusion carries the context beat instead. | Correctly skipped (reads better without) |

## Sentence-level (consistent)

| Move | Instance | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Long descriptive → short **punch** | "But it is not." / "Both." / "This is the pattern." / "To the powerful, a reckoning. To the poor, a rest." | Landed |
| **Conditional/observational openers** | "if you look closely," "At first glance," "Once you have learned to read those two signs" | Landed |
| **Rhythmic triads** | "pirated, copied, imitated"; "moving, dancing, almost jaunty"; "an executioner … a thief … a gentle field-hand" | Landed |
| **Transition signposts** (never hard-cut) | "And yet," "But it is not," "And then you turn to the ploughman," "And that contradiction is exactly where the arguments begin," "So go back and look" | Landed |
| **Two-register diction** (elevated + instant gloss) | *memento mori* → "remember you must die"; *Les Simulachres…* translated; "reformist satire … That is the crucial word. Satire." | Landed |

## Hook & Close (skeleton)
- **Hook:** *Shocking Reaction* variant (documented reception — 100+ pirated editions), pivoting within two sentences to the contrast thesis + the promise "one small cut … quietly overturns everything." Paid off in the Reveal. ✓
- **Close (in order):** reflective summary that keeps the mystery open ("still arguing over whether … justice or … the plain, level truth") → open question to the viewer ("mercy, or a verdict?") → neutral warm sign-off. ✓

## Do / Don't — all hold

| Don't | Status |
|---|---|
| Resolve into one neat answer | HELD — "Both"; ends on an open question |
| Dump biography first | HELD — hook is reception; bio arrives in beat 2 |
| Jargon left unexplained | HELD — *memento mori*, *reformist satire*, title all glossed |
| Break the curator persona | HELD |
| Cynical / mocking toward the art | HELD — appreciative throughout |
| Over-nested unspeakable clauses | HELD — sentences breathe |
| Segments ending flat | HELD — every beat bridges forward |
| Open with "Today we'll look at…" / numbered signposting | HELD — braided, no chapters |

## Do/Don't fixes applied
- **Grammar (speakability):** hook opener "There **was** eleven editions" → "There **were** eleven editions of it before the year 1562, and across the rest of the sixteenth century, perhaps a hundred more." An audible error, not a stylistic choice; corrected in place.

## Clothing — no channel-identity content copied verbatim
- **Sponsor read + coded URL / print-catalog shop pitch (guide appendix commerce layer): SKIPPED entirely.** No monetization, no platform CTA.
- **"Now then, let's continue." (persona catchphrase / ad-bracket): FUNCTION ADAPTED, phrase NOT used.** Asides reset with neutral signposts ("So go back and look…", "But watch who Death comes for…").
- **Warm sign-off: FUNCTION ADAPTED to a neutral close** — "Thank you for sitting with these old pictures a while; there is always another story waiting in the next one." No "I'll see you in the next video," no claimed channel identity.
- **Exemplar Lines:** used only as a cadence tuning-fork (e.g., the "But it is not." punch rhythm) — none transplanted.
