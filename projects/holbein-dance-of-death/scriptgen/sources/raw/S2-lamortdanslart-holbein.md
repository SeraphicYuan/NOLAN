# [S2] lamortdanslart.com — Holbein's Danse macabre
URL: https://www.lamortdanslart.com/danse/Manuscrit/Holbein/dd_holbein.htm
Fetched: 2026-06-25

- Design: circa 1526. Publication: 1538 (page says "Basle"; cf. S1 which says Lyons — DISCREPANCY), 41 woodcuts.
- Expanded editions: 1545 (Lyon, +8 figures by an unknown artist); 1562 (Lyon, 57 woodcuts total).
- Structure: opens with four Genesis scenes and skeletal musicians; **34 victims** in social hierarchy —
  pope, emperor, king, cardinal, empress, queen, bishop, duke, abbot, abbess, nobleman, canon, judge,
  advocate, senator, preacher, priest, monk, nun, old woman, physician, astrologer, rich man, merchant,
  seaman, knight, count, old man, countess, noblewoman, duchess, peddler, **ploughman**, and child;
  concludes with Last Judgment and Death's escutcheon (coat of arms).
- "**Death is turned into a dispenser of justice, denouncing greediness and the abuse of power.**"
- Reformation-era humanism / criticism of clerical excess — BUT "neither the preacher nor the priest
  attract Holbein's sarcasms" (their sympathy with the poor). [Nuance against a blanket anticlerical read.]
- Death's dual character: toward the **ploughman (laboureur)**, Death "helps … to plough his field"
  (assistant, not antagonist); "kidnaps a child" cruelly; "solemnly attends" the elderly to rest;
  nobility faces Death as "invincible"; the rich man is robbed of "money before stealing his soul."
- Symbol system: the **hourglass appears 25 of 35 times**; broken crowns and swords signify failed authority.
