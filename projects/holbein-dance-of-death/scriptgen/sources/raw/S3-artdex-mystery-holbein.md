# [S3] Artdex — "The Mystery of Hans Holbein's Death" (Dance of Death section)
URL: https://www.artdex.com/the-mystery-of-hans-holbeins-death/
Fetched: 2026-06-25

Facts:
- Created during Holbein's time in **Basel, Switzerland (1523–25)**; Holbein in his twenties.
- A series of woodcuts depicting **34 individuals** meeting their end (knight, physician, members
  of the clergy, a plowman, an advocate, an astrologer, …).
- Themes: universal mortality ("Death doesn't choose"), memento mori, social critique.

Interpretive arguments (author's reading, NOT documented artist statements — flagged):
- Death "skewers a knight" and "boldly pulls an abbot," but "takes a gentler route when dealing
  with the less fortunate."
- The author argues this demonstrates Holbein's "social criticism of the wealthy and the Catholic church":
  Death as "kindness" for the poor vs. "horrific punishment" for the corrupt powerful.
- Does NOT name an engraver or give publication details.
