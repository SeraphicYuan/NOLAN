## [S1] Wikipedia — "Danse Macabre" (Holbein section)
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danse_Macabre · fetched 2026-06-25 · ~120 words used
- "Hans Holbein the Younger (1497–1543) created the designs in 1526 while in Basel."
- "Block-cutter: Hans Lützelburger, whose work was praised as 'technically the most marvelous woodcuts ever made' (Ivins, quoting W. J. Linton)."
- "First book edition: *Les Simulachres et Historiées Faces de la Mort*, published at Lyons by the Trechsel brothers in 1538, containing 41 woodcuts."
- "there were eleven editions before 1562, and over the sixteenth century perhaps as many as a hundred unauthorized editions and imitations."
- Holbein refashioned "the late-medieval allegory of the Danse Macabre as a reformist satire," reflecting "a gradual shift toward reformed Christianity."
- "the apparent class distinction … is completely neutralized by Death as the ultimate equalizer."
- "none escape Death's skeletal clutches, not even the pious."

## [S2] lamortdanslart.com — Holbein's Danse macabre
URL: https://www.lamortdanslart.com/danse/Manuscrit/Holbein/dd_holbein.htm · fetched 2026-06-25 · ~150 words used
- Design circa 1526; publication 1538 (page says "Basle" — DISCREPANCY vs [S1] Lyons); 41 woodcuts.
- Expanded editions: 1545 (Lyon, +8 figures by an unknown artist); 1562 (Lyon, 57 woodcuts total).
- Opens with four Genesis scenes and skeletal musicians; 34 victims in social hierarchy (pope … ploughman, and child); concludes with Last Judgment and Death's escutcheon.
- "Death is turned into a dispenser of justice, denouncing greediness and the abuse of power."
- "neither the preacher nor the priest attract Holbein's sarcasms" (their sympathy with the poor).
- Toward the ploughman, Death "helps … to plough his field"; "kidnaps a child" cruelly; "solemnly attends" the elderly to rest; nobility faces Death as "invincible"; the rich man is robbed of "money before stealing his soul."
- "the hourglass appears 25 of 35 times"; broken crowns and swords signify failed authority.

## [S3] Artdex — "The Mystery of Hans Holbein's Death" (Dance of Death section)
URL: https://www.artdex.com/the-mystery-of-hans-holbeins-death/ · fetched 2026-06-25 · ~60 words used
- Created during Holbein's Basel time (dated here 1523–25), in his twenties. [DISCREPANCY vs [S1]/[S2] 1526]
- A series of woodcuts of 34 individuals meeting their end (knight, physician, clergy, a plowman, an advocate, an astrologer …).
- Interpretive (author's reading, NOT a documented artist statement): Death "takes a gentler route when dealing with the less fortunate"; the poor/rich contrast shows "social criticism of the wealthy and the Catholic church."
- Does NOT name an engraver or give publication details.
