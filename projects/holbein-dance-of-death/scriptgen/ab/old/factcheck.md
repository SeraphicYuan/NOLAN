## Claims that could not be fully supported (softened or flagged in script)
- **#4** "finest portraitists of the Northern Renaissance" — model knowledge, presented as descriptive color, not load-bearing fact.
- **#16–#18** Basel-as-humanist-hub, Luther/1517, Holbein's reform circles — all model knowledge; each explicitly softened ("it is generally held that…", "is thought to have…") per the grounded-but-graceful policy. Note S1 does support the broader "reformist satire / shift toward reformed Christianity" framing.
- **#20** "drives the horses on" — extrapolated detail beyond S2's wording ("helps to plough his field"); kept as light description, not a hard claim.
- **Date discrepancy:** S1 = 1526; S2 = c.1526; S3 = 1523–25. Script uses "mid-1520s" to avoid overcommitting.
- **Place discrepancy:** S2's page attaches 1538 to "Basle"; S1 attributes the 1538 edition to Lyons. Script follows S1 (Lyons).
