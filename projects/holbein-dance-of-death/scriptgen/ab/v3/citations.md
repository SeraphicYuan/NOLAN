## [S2] lamortdanslart.com — Holbein's Danse macabre
URL: https://www.lamortdanslart.com/danse/Manuscrit/Holbein/dd_holbein.htm · fetched 2026-06-25 · file: `sources/raw/S2-lamortdanslart-holbein.md`
- Design c.1526; publication 1538 (page says "Basle" — discrepancy vs S1); 41 woodcuts; 1545 & 1562 expanded editions.
- Structure: four Genesis scenes + skeletal musicians; **34 victims** listed top-down "pope, emperor, king… peddler, ploughman, and child"; Last Judgment + Death's escutcheon.
- "Death is turned into a dispenser of justice, denouncing greediness and the abuse of power."
- "neither the preacher nor the priest attract Holbein's sarcasms" (sympathy with the poor).
- Ploughman: Death "helps … to plough his field"; "kidnaps a child"; "solemnly attends" the elderly; nobility Death is "invincible"; rich man robbed of "money before stealing his soul."
- "the hourglass appears 25 of 35 times"; "broken crowns and swords signify failed authority."

## [S3] Artdex — "The Mystery of Hans Holbein's Death"
URL: https://www.artdex.com/the-mystery-of-hans-holbeins-death/ · fetched 2026-06-25 · file: `sources/raw/S3-artdex-mystery-holbein.md`
- Made in Basel, Switzerland, 1523–25; Holbein in his twenties; 34 individuals (knight, physician, clergy, plowman, advocate, astrologer…).
- Death "skewers a knight," "boldly pulls an abbot," but "takes a gentler route when dealing with the less fortunate."
- Author's interpretive reading (flagged, not documented artist intent): "social criticism of the wealthy and the Catholic church" — kindness for the poor vs. punishment for the corrupt powerful.
