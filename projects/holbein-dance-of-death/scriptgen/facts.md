## HOOK beat facts — CONCRETE-FIRST (documented, dated, quotable → cold-open candidates)
- The little book of death images became a runaway hit: **eleven editions before 1562**, and across the 16th century **perhaps a hundred unauthorized editions and imitations** — src:[S1] · purpose:hook · beat:hook · conf:verified · role:supports
- First book edition — ***Les Simulachres et Historiées Faces de la Mort*** — carried **41 woodcuts**, published at **Lyon by the Trechsel brothers in 1538** — src:[S1] · purpose:hook · beat:hook · conf:verified · role:supports
- The tradition's whole point, quoted: **"the apparent class distinction … is completely neutralized by Death as the ultimate equalizer"** — src:[S1] · purpose:hook · beat:hook · conf:verified · role:supports
- The block-cutter's work was praised as **"technically the most marvelous woodcuts ever made"** (Ivins, quoting W. J. Linton) — src:[S1] · purpose:authority · beat:hook · conf:verified · role:supports

## CONTEXT beat — the work & its making
- Designed by **Hans Holbein the Younger (1497–1543)** while living in **Basel** — src:[S1,S3] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- Date of design: **1526** per Wikipedia; **1523–25** per Artdex (Holbein "in his twenties") — src:[S1,S3] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:claimed · role:context
- Cut in wood by **Hans Lützelburger**, a master block-cutter — src:[S1] · purpose:authority · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports
- Lützelburger **died in 1526, before the series was published** — src:[needs-check] · purpose:humanize · beat:context · conf:claimed · role:context — NOT in fetched sources; widely reported elsewhere → soften/attribute, never state flatly
- DISCREPANCY on place of first publication: [S1] says **Lyon (Trechsel brothers)**; [S2] says **"Basle."** Use Lyon (more specific/authoritative); note conflict — src:[S1,S2] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:disputed · role:context
- Each block is tiny — figure roughly **6.5 cm, smaller than a playing card** — src:[needs-check] · purpose:contrast · beat:context · conf:claimed · role:supports — keep vague or drop
- Expanded later editions: **1545** (Lyon, +8 figures by an unknown artist); **1562** (Lyon, **57 woodcuts** total) — src:[S2] · purpose:proof · beat:context · conf:verified · role:supports

## EVIDENCE beat — structure & how Death behaves (the tonal engine)
- Opens with **four Genesis scenes + skeletal musicians**; closes with the **Last Judgment** and **Death's escutcheon (coat of arms)** — src:[S2] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- **34 victims in descending social hierarchy**: pope, emperor, king, cardinal, empress, queen, bishop, duke, abbot, abbess, nobleman, canon, judge, advocate, senator, preacher, priest, monk, nun, physician, astrologer, rich man, merchant, seaman, knight, count, ploughman, child … — src:[S2,S3] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- The **hourglass** recurs as Death's prop — **25 of 35 times**; **broken crowns and swords** signify failed authority — src:[S2] · purpose:proof · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Death is a **"dispenser of justice, denouncing greediness and the abuse of power"** — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Toward the powerful: nobility faces Death as **"invincible"**; the **rich man** is robbed of his **"money before stealing his soul"** — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports
- Death **"boldly pulls an abbot"** and **"skewers a knight"** — src:[S3] · purpose:contrast · beat:evidence · conf:verified · role:supports

## TURN beat — THE PLOUGHMAN PIVOT (the hidden-detail reveal)
- Toward the **ploughman (laboureur)**, Death **"helps … to plough his field"** — an assistant, not an antagonist — src:[S2] · purpose:hook · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports ← THE PIVOT
- Death **"takes a gentler route when dealing with the less fortunate"** — src:[S3] · purpose:contrast · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports
- Death **"solemnly attends"** the elderly to their rest, but **"kidnaps a child"** cruelly — src:[S2] · purpose:humanize · beat:turn · conf:verified · role:supports

## CLOSE beat — competing interpretations & recontextualization
- Traditional reading: **memento mori** + **Death as the great equalizer**, class distinction "completely neutralized" — src:[S1] · purpose:authority · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- Reformist reading: Holbein refashioned the medieval Danse Macabre as a **"reformist satire,"** reflecting a shift toward reformed Christianity — src:[S1] · purpose:authority · beat:close · conf:verified · role:supports
- The **"social criticism of the wealthy and the Catholic church"** — Death as kindness for the poor, punishment for the corrupt powerful — src:[S3] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:claimed · role:supports — author's interpretation, flagged (not a documented Holbein statement)
- COUNTER / steelman: **"neither the preacher nor the priest attract Holbein's sarcasms"** — his lower clergy are shown sympathetic to the poor — src:[S2] · purpose:contrast · beat:close · conf:verified · role:challenges — complicates a blanket "anticlerical" read; keeps ambiguity honest
