## Key supporting quotes (by claim)

- **Seven cities / blindness doubt** — [S4]: "Seven Cities claimed Homer as their Citizen and is said to have been blind although the richness of his visual imagery renders this doubtful."
- **Forgery charge** — [S3]: "他们宣称 古希腊的《荷马史诗》是后人的伪作 因为西方学术界根本就无法确认 荷马是真实存在的" (they claim the epics are a later forgery, since Western scholarship cannot confirm Homer really existed).
- **Demodocus / the Muse** — [S4]: "she ripped him of his eyes but she gave him the sweet singing art."
- **Demodocus sings, Odysseus weeps** — [S2]: "the poet's attention would turn to the tale of troy… of the famous odysseus the maker of the wooden horse… odysseus thinking only of the many friends he had lost would grab his robe and begin to weep."
- **Oral tradition** — [S4]: "Homer had the story and many of the lines of the Odyssey handed down to him by generations of oral poets."
- **Yugoslav singers** — [S4]: "oral poets like demodicus could be found at the beginning of this Century in the former Yugoslavia singing without the aid of any written notes poems as long as the Iliad or the Odyssey."
- **Epithets** — [S4]: "Dawn with her Rosy fingers and gray eyed athene"; [S3]: "「捷足的阿喀琉斯」" (swift-footed Achilles).
- **24 books / 400 years** — [S4]: "The Odyssey was divided into 24 books corresponding to the 24 letters of the Greek alphabet 400 years after its composition… this division is not the work of Homer."
- **Feigned madness / plough** — [S1]: "plowing his fields with salt… placed the infant directly in the path of the plow. Without hesitation, Odysius yanked the plow aside."
- **Cyclops / Nobody / ram** — [S2]: "instead of giving his true name odysseus would insist that he was called nobody… plunge the stake deep into his eye… tie themselves to the bottom of the sheep."
- **Boast → curse** — [S2]: "reveal to the cyclops that his name was… odysseus… would curse odysseus to lose all his crew… and wander the seas for ten long years."
- **Sirens** — [S2]: "instruct his crew to plug their ears with beeswax… asking his crew to bind him to the ship's mast."
- **Athena vs. wits** — [S4]: "it is sometimes difficult to distinguish between the goddess of wisdom and Odysseus's own quick wits as the source of his inspiration."
- **Monsters confined to Books 9–12** — [S4]: "they only appear in books 9 to 12… about a sixth of it… the story that Odysseus himself tells."
- **Schliemann 1873 / Calvert** — [S3]: "1873年 施里曼在这里发现了一批黄金银器… 普里阿摩斯的宝藏"; "英国裔的外交官… 弗兰克·卡尔福特 他早就注意到… 特洛伊城所在的位置."
- **Ventris 1952 / Linear B** — [S3]: "到了1952年… 迈克尔·文特里斯 就成功破译了线性文字B… 记录的 就是一种早期希腊语."
- **Wolf 1795** — [S3]: "直到1795年… 沃尔夫 出版了《荷马导论》… 源自于更早的口头吟唱传统… 由许多分散的史诗歌段逐渐积累."
- **Between myth, literature, memory** — [S3]: "它位于神话、文学和历史记忆之间."
- **Complexity ≠ forgery** — [S3]: "版本复杂不意味着文本就一定是伪作… 我们会不会因为《红楼梦》存在版本争议 就说《红楼梦》是假的呢… 当然不会."
- **Penelope's shroud** — [S2]: "weaving a burial garment for odysseus's father… every night… she would secretly undo all her work… lasting for three years."
