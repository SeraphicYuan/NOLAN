# Candidate Angles — Homer / The Odyssey

Style note: the "Great Books Explained" channel's one non-negotiable move is **biographical determinism** — use the author's real life as the master key that unlocks the fiction. Homer is the rare subject where that key *cannot turn*: there may be no single author to find. Each angle below is scored partly on how it handles that problem and the S3 forgery counter-source.

---

**Angle 1 — The Odyssey has no author, and that absence is the key that unlocks it.**
The channel's usual master key — the writer's life explaining the work — fails here: "Homer" may be not a man but centuries of oral singers. So the "life" that determines this text is the *tradition itself* — and the poem's hero, Odysseus the man of many turns, disguises and borrowed names, mirrors the many-voiced tradition that made him. A hero who is never one fixed identity, sung into being by an author who was never one fixed person.
- *supported by:* [S3] (Wolf 1795, oral-formulaic theory, Linear B) · [S4] (oral poets, Demodocus, formulas, "man" as first word)
- *tension it must handle:* if there was no single Homer, doesn't that prove the forgery charge (S3) — that the epics are a late fake?
- *why compelling:* it turns the style's signature move into the story's own drama, and the counter-source becomes the engine, not a footnote.

**Angle 2 — One reckless boast cost Odysseus ten years and every man he led.**
Cause → effect. Odysseus survives the Cyclops by erasing his name ("Nobody"), then cannot resist reclaiming it — and that single act of pride hands Poseidon his curse. The whole homecoming is the price of needing to be known.
- *supported by:* [S1][S2] (the Nobody trick and fatal name-reveal; Poseidon's curse) · [S4]
- *tension:* the same cunning that dooms him ("Nobody") is what saves him — trait and flaw are one.
- *why compelling:* a tight, tragic mechanism a general viewer feels instantly.

**Angle 3 — The most famous monsters in Western literature are a story Odysseus tells about himself.**
Hidden detail. Cyclops, Circe, Sirens, Scylla — the images everyone remembers — appear only in books 9–12, the four books narrated by Odysseus, in his own voice, to an audience he wants to impress. Homer quarantines the fantastic inside first-person testimony.
- *supported by:* [S4] (monsters confined to the hero's own narration; a different register)
- *tension:* is Homer flagging an unreliable narrator, or simply shifting poetic register?
- *why compelling:* a genuine "I never noticed that" reframe of the best-known part of the poem.

**Angle 4 — The Odyssey is the first great story to argue that coming home beats being a hero.**
Reframe of theme. Against the Iliad's death-or-glory, the Odyssey's dead Achilles would rather be a living slave than king of the underworld. The epic's true prize is not conquest but a bed, a wife, a son, an old dog.
- *supported by:* [S4] (Achilles' shade; the Iliad/Odyssey contrast; xenia; like-mindedness)
- *tension:* yet the homecoming ends in the slaughter of 108 suitors — peace bought with a massacre.
- *why compelling:* warm, humane, and true to the style's contemplative close.

**Angle 5 — Odysseus's real equal isn't a monster — it's Penelope.**
Reframe. The cleverest man in Greece meets his match in the wife who unweaves a shroud for years and traps him with the secret of their own bed. Two minds of identical cunning; "like-mindedness" as the poem's ideal.
- *supported by:* [S1][S2] (shroud trick, bed test) · [S4] (homophrosyne; Clytemnestra contrast)
- *tension:* the poem still names itself for the man, and gives her far fewer lines.
- *why compelling:* corrects the "hero-and-his-loyal-wife" cliché with a genuine partnership read.

---

## Chosen

**[CHOSEN] Angle 1 — The Odyssey has no author, and that absence is the key that unlocks it.**

It wins on both axes. On **evidence**, it is the only angle whose spine *is* the strongest scholarly material we have — S3's Homeric Question (Wolf, Schliemann, Linear B, oral-formulaic theory) and S4's account of oral composition (Demodocus, the formulas, "man" as the first word) — so the mandated counter-source stops being a threat to answer and becomes the argument itself. On **resonance**, it does something only this subject allows: it takes the channel's defining biographical-determinism move, shows it breaking, and redirects it — the "author's life" that explains the poem becomes the life of the *tradition*, mirrored in a hero who is himself a shape-shifter of names and disguises. The forgery charge (no single Homer ⇒ fake) is met head-on: absence of one author is not forgery but the fingerprint of oral making — proven by Troy in the ground and Greek on the Linear B tablets.
