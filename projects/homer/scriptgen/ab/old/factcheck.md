## Beat 3 — The World That Sang It

- Fixed formulae: "rosy-fingered Dawn," "grey-eyed Athena," "resourceful Odysseus." → `[S4]` *"Dawn with her Rosy fingers and gray eyed athene."* / `[S3]` *"「足智多谋的奥德修斯」"* (resourceful/much-devising Odysseus).
- Epithets are "machinery" for memory and rhythm, not clumsiness. → `[S3]` *"它们的作用 是帮助诗人进行长时间的记忆 也可以帮助诗句之间保持节奏感"* (they help the poet's long memory and keep the rhythm of the lines).
- A century ago, singers in the Balkans improvised poems as long as the Odyssey with no text. → `[S4]` *"oral poets like demodicus could be found at the beginning of this Century in the former Yugoslavia singing without the aid of any written notes poems as long as the Iliad or the Odyssey."*
- The Troy of the poem stood ~1200 BC. → `[S4]` *"the wanderings of Heroes like Odysseus in the Years between these events occurring around 1200 BC."*
- Schliemann, businessman/treasure-hunter, led there by Englishman Frank Calvert, dug Troy in what is now Turkey. → `[S3]` *"施里曼并不是今天意义上 那些训练有素的专业考古学家 他原本就是一个成功的商人 … 有一位英国裔的外交官 叫做弗兰克·卡尔福特 … 在土耳其西北部的西萨尔勒克 发现了特洛伊城的遗址."*
- Linear B decoded 1952; tablets are palace ledgers (grain, livestock, offerings); prove a real world of kings/palaces/records. → `[S3]` *"到了1952年 … 迈克尔·文特里斯 就成功破译了线性文字B … 记录的大多是一些宫殿行政的档案 比如粮食土地 牲畜、祭品."*
- "not history, but not pure invention — memory, sung into shape." → `[S3]` *"它不是现代的历史书 … 但它也不是毫无历史根据的童话 它位于神话、文学和历史记忆之间"* + `[interpretation]` (phrasing).
