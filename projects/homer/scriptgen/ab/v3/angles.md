# Spine Type + Candidate Angles — Homer / The Odyssey (v3)

## Inferred spine TYPE (from the constitution)

Reading the guide's **Narrative Structure** ("Author biography — the pivotal life event. *This is the spine*"), the **braiding rule** ("biographical determinism / mirroring… every life fact cashed out as a fictional element"), the **master device** ("every script is organized around a central opposition / contrast"), and the **Hook Patterns** (all resolve into "the familiar/shocking/current, then ground it in the book and a real human life") —

**Spine type this channel builds on:** *a single felt human through-line — one life mirrored into the work, organized around one emotional contrast — carried as a story a general viewer feels, never as an intellectual debate.* The spine is always **a person and a longing**, not a **question about the text**.

**The Homer problem:** the usual master key (the author's life) *cannot turn* — there may be no single Homer to find. The *wrong* response (the earlier run) is to promote that gap into the spine and argue "is the *Odyssey* real, or a forgery, and who wrote it?" — an authorship *debate*. That is the wrong spine **type** (a question, not a longing), low-resonance, and it forces exactly the isolated meta/lecture beats the guide's DON'Ts forbid. The right response is to keep the spine a human longing and **redirect the mirroring**: the poem about a man desperate to be *known* was made by a maker we can never name.

All angles below are OF THAT TYPE (a felt human through-line). Scores 1–5: **Resonance** (will a general viewer *feel* it?), **Evidence**, **Style-fit** (deliverable through the guide's braided arc + pacing, no isolated lecture beats).

**Angle A — Home over glory: the first great story that says being *known* by the people you love beats being a legend.**
Odysseus could have been pure legend — the man who took Troy. Instead he lies, suffers, and claws through ten years of sea for one thing: to sit in the dark beside his wife and be *known* again. The *Iliad* worships death-and-glory; the *Odyssey* answers *stay alive, come home, endure.* The mirroring move, redirected: the poem about a man fighting to be remembered outlived the one poet history forgot — Homer survives exactly as Odysseus did, as a voice, "Nobody" and everybody, still singing us home.
- *supports:* [S2][S1] longing for Ithaca; [S4] Achilles' shade, Agamemnon contrast, "stay alive," plank; [S1] olive-tree bed, scar-recognition; [S3][S4] the vanished author redirected as the mirror.
- *contrast (device):* glory (Iliad/Achilles) vs. home (Odyssey/Odysseus).
- **Resonance 5 · Evidence 5 · Style-fit 5**

**Angle B — Odysseus survives by becoming "Nobody," but nearly dies of the need to be *Somebody*.**
The "Nobody" trick in the Cyclops's cave saves him; his pride in shouting his real name curses him to ten more years. A tight tragic mechanism about the hunger to be known.
- *supports:* [S1][S2] Nobody / name-boast / Poseidon's curse; [S4].
- *contrast:* self-erasure vs. self-assertion.
- **Resonance 4 · Evidence 4 · Style-fit 4** — excellent, but narrower than A; folds into A as the mid-poem turn.

**Angle C — Odysseus's true equal isn't a monster — it's Penelope.**
Two minds of identical cunning: he lies his way home, she unweaves a shroud for three years and traps him with the secret of their own bed. "Like-mindedness" as the poem's ideal.
- *supports:* [S1] shroud, bed-test, homophrosyne; [S4] Clytemnestra contrast.
- *contrast:* the many women who trap him (Circe/Calypso/Sirens) vs. the one who waits.
- **Resonance 4 · Evidence 4 · Style-fit 4** — warm and true; woven into A's home-stakes and close rather than the whole spine.

**Angle D — The most famous monsters in Western literature are a story Odysseus tells about himself.**
Cyclops, Circe, Sirens appear only in Books 9–12, narrated by Odysseus to an audience he wants to impress — Homer quarantines the fantastic inside first-person testimony.
- *supports:* [S4].
- *contrast:* the hero's tall tale vs. the poem's realism.
- **Resonance 3 · Evidence 3 · Style-fit 3** — a genuine "I never noticed that," but a *device*, not a felt through-line; used as one beat.

**Angle E — Is the *Odyssey* real, or a late forgery — and did Homer even exist?** *(WRONG-TYPE — retained only to name what v3 rejects.)*

Low resonance (a general viewer doesn't ache over textual recension), and it can only be delivered as isolated meta/lecture beats, violating the guide's DON'Ts ("no isolated analysis-only sections"). This was the earlier run's spine and it hurt retention.
- **Resonance 2 · Evidence 5 · Style-fit 2 → REJECTED as spine.** Its strong evidence is *reassigned* to the HOOK + one accelerated fact-cluster inside Angle A.

## Chosen

**[CHOSEN] Angle A — Home over glory: being *known* by the people you love beats being a legend.**

It wins on **resonance** (everyone has ached for home, or to be known by someone who has waited — no classics degree required) and on **style-fit**: it is a felt human through-line built on the guide's master contrast (glory vs. home), it lands the guide's mandated *contemplative close* on the olive-tree bed, and it *rescues* the broken biographical-mirroring move instead of abandoning it — the unfindable author becomes the final turn ("the poem about a man fighting to be known outlived the poet we can't name"). Crucially, it lets the forgery/authorship evidence (Angle E's only strength) do real work as the **shock hook** and one fast fact-cluster that *grounds* the mirror — present, honest, retained — without ever becoming the dry debate that sank the earlier run.
