# Beat-Map — Homer / The Odyssey (v3)

Chosen spine: **home over glory — being *known* beats being a legend** (biographical-mirroring redirected onto the vanished author).
Constitution used as HARD constraints: guide's Hook Patterns, braided Narrative Structure (Hook → Thesis → "biography"[spine] → context → plot/cast → thematic heart → legacy → contemplative close), Pacing (accelerate on facts/lists, decelerate on emotion/synthesis), and the DON'Ts (no isolated analysis-only sections, no "First/Second," don't resolve symbols, don't debunk).

**Retention design:** open on the sanctioned SHOCK hook (the forgery charge) to spike curiosity in the first 15 seconds; convert that spike immediately into a *human* thesis so the viewer is chasing feeling, not scholarship; keep the middle moving with two accelerated fact-clusters (oral tradition; archaeology) braided into the emotion; then run a long decelerate through the back third — adventures → the glory-vs-home argument → the reunion — so the curve *rises into feeling* exactly where retention usually sags. The S3 forgery material lives ONLY in Beat 1 (hook) and Beat 3 (accelerated cluster); it never becomes a lecture beat.

---

## 1 · Hook — "the storyteller we can't prove existed" · pace:accelerate · covers:[S3][S4] · serves-spine: SHOCK hook (guide type 3) + paradox; states the forgery charge in one breath, then pivots within two sentences to the poem and a *human* question — who was so desperate to get home that we're still telling his story 3,000 years later?

## 2 · Thesis / stakes — "home over glory" · pace:decelerate · covers:[S2][S4][S1] · serves-spine: the flat confident thesis (guide type 5) of what it's REALLY about — not monsters, not gods, but a man who could have been a legend choosing instead to claw home to be *known*. Plant the master contrast (Iliad=glory / Odyssey=home) and the feigned-madness detail so home-over-glory is his character from line one.

## 3 · The maker we can't find — mirroring, redirected · pace:accelerate · covers:[S4][S3][S1] · serves-spine: the guide's biography-is-the-spine beat, adapted — since there's no single Homer, the "life" behind the poem is the oral tradition. ACCELERATED FACT-CLUSTER (this is where the forgery material is answered, not debated): Wolf 1795, the fixed epithets as memory-machinery, Balkan singers, Demodocus the blind bard inside the poem. Land on: absence of one author isn't forgery — it's the fingerprint of a song kept alive by many mouths.

## 4 · The world in the ground · pace:accelerate · covers:[S3] · serves-spine: the guide's historical-context beat + quantification-for-awe. Fast cluster: Schliemann/Calvert and Troy at Hisarlik; Linear B decoded by Ventris in 1952. Settle on the honest middle — "between myth, literature and remembered history." (Braids straight out of Beat 3; no separate "is it real?" section.)

## 5 · Ithaca is being eaten — the home worth the war · pace:decelerate · covers:[S1][S2] · serves-spine: the guide's plot-premise + cast beat, kept emotional. Twenty years gone; a hundred suitors devouring the hall; Penelope's shroud unpicked by lamplight; Telemachus grown among strangers. THIS is what he's fighting to reach — a wife, a son, a home.

## 6 · Nobody — survival, and the cost of needing to be known · pace:accelerate · covers:[S1][S2][S4] · serves-spine: the analytical heart, braided as story (folds in Angles B & D). The monsters live only in Odysseus's own telling; the "Nobody" trick saves him; the name-boast curses him. Reframe the "flaw" (his men's deaths) as the poem's point: he endures by self-control, they don't. Leave the register open (is he an unreliable narrator or shifting poetic gear?) — don't resolve it.

## 7 · The argument in one breath — home beats glory · pace:decelerate · covers:[S4][S1] · serves-spine: the emotional/philosophical synthesis (deep decelerate). The master contrast pays off: Agamemnon's fast, proud, fatal homecoming vs. Odysseus's slow, secret, faithful one (Penelope vs. Clytemnestra); Achilles' shade would rather be a living slave than king of the dead. The plank after the shipwreck — greatness as stubborn survival, not the sword.

## 8 · Known again — the reunion · pace:decelerate · covers:[S1][S4] · serves-spine: the contemplative close begins on the deepest emotion. The dog Argos; the nurse and the boar-scar; the bow through twelve axes; and the olive-tree bed that cannot be moved — a marriage literally grown from the ground. To come home is to be *known* by those who waited.

## 9 · The voice that never came home · pace:decelerate → settle · covers:[S4][S3] · serves-spine: the guide's modern-relevance + image-of-mystery close, and the redirected-mirroring turn. From Beowulf to the road movie to the superhero, every long road home walks Homer's path. Final image: the poem about a man who fought to be known outlived the poet we can never name — Homer survives as Odysseus did, as "Nobody" and everybody, a voice still singing us home. Leave it open, not summarized.
